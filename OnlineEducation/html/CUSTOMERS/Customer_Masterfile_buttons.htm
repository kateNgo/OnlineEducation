Customer Masterfile buttons

 

 

CUSTOMER MASTERFILE BUTTONS

 

Defaults Button - The default customer settings option allows you to configure a range of specific setting for a certain type of customer. These settings can then be grouped so that whenever a new customer is added, the new customer can take on the same attributes as other similar customers. For example your trade customers would have certain discount and price structures, belong to certain groups and territories and have other customer options in common. Rather than manually setting these every time a new customer is added, you can set up a default customer type and have these setting automatically apply to all of these customers.

 

Under the Tools Menu from within AM-Win you are able to select the Default Settings for Customers option. This option then allows you to insert, change or delete a default customer type.

 

To add a new default customer type, click on the insert button or press the INSERT key.

 

You are then prompted to add a Default Group code and description, which can then be used when adding a new customer through the Customer Masterfiles or when adding a customer on the fly.

 

Enter the information including group, territory, mail group, account terms, price rates, discount rates and so on that you wish to use for this default customer type. Refer to the Customer Masterfiles for clarification on these fields if necessary.

 

You can then change or delete a customer default type using the change or delete keys.

 

The next step is to add a new customer and apply these settings to that customer. From the Customer Masterfile screen you must first insert a customer in the usual way. After entering the customercode, name and address you can then click on the defaults button and select the type of customer you are entering. The customer being added will then automatically take on all of the default attributes of that default customer type. This not only saves you time it also means that you are more consistent and efficient with the way your customers are configured.

 

You can also select the default customer type when adding a customer on the fly from the invoicing modules.

 

Job Invoice Button - F2  Pressing the F2 key or clicking on this button will open up a new invoice for the selected customer.

 

Enquiry Button - F3  Pressing the F3 key or clicking on this button will open up a new enquiries screen with the selected customer open for the current month.

 

Payment Button - F4 - Pressing the F4 key or clicking on this button will open up a new payments screen for the selected customer showing all outstanding invoices to be paid.

 

Payment Enquiry Button  F5  Pressing the F5 key or clicking on this button will open a new window displaying details of all of the payments that have been previously made by this customer.

 

Job Item Button  F6 - Pressing the F6 key or clicking on this button will open a new window displaying details of all of the job items (vehicles, boats, lawnmowers etc) that are owned by this customer.

 

Sales Button - F7 - Pressing the F7 key or clicking on this button will open a new window displaying all of the sales of stock or revenue items that have been sold to this customer.

 

Hist Button - Clicking on this button will open a customer history window allowing you to view all of the invoice history for this customer.

 

RPT Button - Clicking on this button will open a new report window allowing you to view a report of the invoice history for this customer.

 

Aged Analysis Button  F8 - Pressing the F8 key or clicking on this button will open a new window allowing you to print a debtors aged analysis for all customers with an outstanding balance and not just the selected customer.

 

Rpt  Ctrl P Button - This button can be used to print out a report for All customers, Detail Single customers, a Group of customers or customers with Balances Owing. You can then select the required Group or customer as applicable.

 

Rpt 2  Ctrl R Button : This button can be used to print out details for all customers, a single customer, a group of customers, or customers with balances owing.

 

When you press the print button, the Customer Report Options window appears.

 

Left click on the down arrow next to the Prepare Type field, and choose from Letter: prints out a standard letter for customers, Label: makes labels for the customers Listing: print out a list of the customers with their details

 

If you choose Letter, a Standard Letter field will appear. Left click the button next to this field, and select the letter you want to send out.

 

Note: these letters are set up in Letter Maintenance under the Others menu.

 

Left click on the arrow next to the From field and choose from Detail Single, Group, Mail Group, Territory, All or Balance.

 

If you choose Detail Single a new field will appear called Select Customer. Left click on the button next to this new field, and select the customer who you want a label/letter/listing for. Then press the OK key.

 

If you choose Group a new field called Select GROUP will appear. If you left click on the button next to this new field, you can select the group of customers you want a label/letter/list for.

 

If you choose Mail Group a new field called Select Mail will appear. If you left click on the button next to this new field, you can select the mail group of customers you want a label/letter/list for.

 

If you choose Territory a new field called Select Territory will appear. If you left click on the button next to this new field, you can select the group of customers you want a label/letter/list for.

 

All: if you choose All, and then click on the OK key, you will have a report with all the customers details (ie: address, phone number) as well as their term details (30 days, 60 days etc), and their YTD and MTD turnovers.

 

Note: these groups can be set up using the Group Maintenance section in the Others menu. Then left click the OK button.

 

If you choose Balance you will have a labels/letters/list for all the customers who have balances. Left click the OK button.

 

You can also double click on the various options to tick and filter down further your report information. You can Include Active customers in your report, Include Suspended customers in your report, Include Contact people and also Include Internal customers as well.

 

DELETE CUSTOMER

 

To delete a customer file choose Delete. All the customer and service history for that customer and motor vehicle history (if any) will also be deleted. If a customer is deleted the system will ask you to enter the reason for the deletion. See the Log File on Delete section of this manual.

 

Note: To delete a customer file, the balance owing by that customer must be nil ($0.00).

 

When a customer or supplier is deleted the system will now prompt you to mark the customer as suspended rather than actually deleting the customer. However, if you chose to delete the customer you may still do so.
