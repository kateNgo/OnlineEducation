Credit Returns

 

CREDIT RETURNS

By default, credit returns automatically get allocated to the customeraccount. However, you may require to give the customer a refund by cash, cheque, credit card or some other method in the event that they have already paid for the invoice and require a refund.

In order to do this, you will need to change the credit method to something other than account.

If The Credit Method Is Not Account Do The Following:

This option is used when the Amount you wish to credit is to be refunded to the customer in the form of CASH, CHEQUE, EFT, CREDIT CARD etc. This will reduce the cash, bank or credit card ledger by the refund amount and return any parts on the credit back into stock.

 

For example, a customer purchases some parts and pays for them. However they come back later and tells you that they didn't need the parts and you decide to credit the original invoice and give them their money back.

Note: If the customer has a zero balance and you use the Account method, then the amount you refund will be treated as an account credit and you will finish up with an unallocated credit amount on that particular account.

After selecting your customer and your invoice or parts or revenue details, Left click on the Process button and the transaction is now complete. You can print out a receipt if you wish.

When the credit return is processed, if you selected a cheque as the refund method you will be required to enter the cheque details later to be made available when you are doing your bank reconciliation.

 

You will also be asked if the cheque has been banked so that the system knows whether to take the money from the bank account or the undeposited cheque account.

 

Please note that the question is asking whether you have banked the money through the software and not whether you have physically banked it or not.

 

Select Invoice to return item

 

When a credit return is done for a customer and the part is manually selected, you are given the opportunity to select which invoice to credit that part from. This allows you to reconcile the return of parts against the original invoice and ensure that a customer doesnreturn parts to you that were purchased from another supplier.

 

Credit Type (POS or JOB)

 

If you have a specific requirement to separate your job and point of sale invoices, then you will also want to separate credit returns that will effect the total sales within these two areas. When doing a credit return, if you select the invoice to credit, the system will automatically recognise what type of invoice it was and credit it appropriately. However, if you do not select the invoice to be credited, you can manually change the credit type to choose either a job or POS credit. By default, the system nominates JOB as the credit type.
