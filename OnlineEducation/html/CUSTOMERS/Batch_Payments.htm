BATCH PAYMENTS

 

Customer batch payments allows you to receipt several payments from several customers in one batch entry process.

 

Select the Batch entry option by left clicking under the main menu of Customers > Payments > Batch Payments. The Customer Payments by Age or Batch screen appears.

 

First, you must locate the customer you wish to receipt a payment for by typing in the first few letters of the customers code into the top locator field or by using the arrow keys to scroll down to find your customer. .

 

Once the customer is found you can then press the insert key on your keyboard or left click on the insert button. The date will then automatically be defaulted to todaydate. You can change the date manually by clicking in the date field or by selecting the relevant date from the calendar by clicking on the Date button above the field.

 

Method  Click on the Method button above method field and select the method of payment or choose from the menu by left clicking Others > Maintenance > Method. The Method File window appears. Use the locator field to select the method of payment or alternatively you can select a method from the menu by left clicking on the payment method and then pressing Select.

 

Select the Age of the payment amount owing that is being paid. If the amount owing covers more than one age then select the oldest age.

 

Note: batch payments will automatically pay from the oldest to the newest invoice in the age you have selected. If you do not wish to pay certain invoices then you must use the open item payments option.

 

Description  Enter description, eg. Account Payment, July account, Bank Branch details etc

 

Cheque/Ref  Enter cheque reference or details, if any. If the payment was a direct deposit then you may wish to enter the bank statement page or some other identifying reference.

 

Amount  Enter the amount received by the customer or to be allocated to the payment and then press enter or click on the update button. This will move the record into the bottom of the screen.

 

Select the next customer to receipt a payment from and then click the insert button to record the next payment.

 

To change an entry, highlight the entry you wish to amend from the bottom screen, then amend the details in the fields above, ie, Description, Cheque/Ref, Amount etc and when you are finished making the changes, make sure you click on the Update button to then save your amendments!!

 

When all records are entered you can then click on the Process button or press the F12 key to process the records. The system will tell you that the process is completed. Press OK.

 

An option to print a payment report of your batch payments after processing is given on the screen. To deactivate this function, click on the tick box to the left of the screen to remove this tick option.

 

Details Button The Details button at the bottom of the screen shows all invoices for the selected customer including paid, part paid and unpaid invoices.

 

Enquiry button  The Enquiry button at the top of the screen allows you to do an enquiry on the customer, supplier or general ledgers.

 

Calculator button  By pressing on the calculator at the top of the screen this activates the calculator function if needed.
