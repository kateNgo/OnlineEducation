PARTS Invoicing

CUSTOMER ORDERS

 

This option is used for invoices that do not have specific Job Items such as for Counter Parts Sales or Non-Service and Warranty types of work.

 

TO CREATE A PARTS ORDER OR ESTIMATE:

 

In the AM-WIN window, click on the Parts Invoicing icon (2nd icon from the left) or from the menu choose Customers, left click and choose Parts Invoicing, left click.

 

The Customer Order screen will appear. At the top left hand corner of the screen, you have a field called Document Type and a dropdown arrow option. If you click on the dropdown arrow you then have the choice to create an ORDER or to create an ESTIMATE.

 

Then to create a new sales invoice or a new estimate, click on New or press F9.

 

Customer  This will automatically default to Cash Only Clients. To change the customer details, refer to the Job Invoicing Chapter.

 

Note: If a customer has been entered in error, click on the Customer button and then select from the Select Customer screen the appropriate customer. Click on Select button and the new customer details will appear in the orders screen.

 

Import Button  click on the Import button if you wish to import a file from another directory into this customer order.

 

Client Alt-C - This button will allow you to edit your customer details on the fly without having to change the details through the Customer Masterfiles screen.

 

Bill To  Alt B  This button will allow you to select another Bill To customer for this sales invoice.

 

Price Rates  This button allows a quick reference for you to check the price rates applicable to this customer if needed.

 

Location  This button allows you to select the location where this sales invoice will be allocated to.

 

Job Item  This button allows you to select the job item details from the make and model list.

 

Desc  This button allows you to change the description on this sales invoice or estimate.

 

Cust Order # - You can enter into this field a customer reference order number applicable to this sales invoice.

 

History Ctrl H  This button allows you to have direct access to the Customer Sales History screen.

 

Send SMS  This button allows you to send an SMS to your customer or nominated contact in relation to this sales invoice.

 

Click on the Send SMS Button. The SMS Message Manager screen will appear. Select the customer contact you wish to send a message to by clicking on the Customer button, choose your job item, type your message, select the phone number you wish to use to send your message to and tick the Reply to button to allow your response to be received at another address (ie email address). Then click on Send SMS.

 

Create PO  This button will allow you to create a purchase order based on the part information you have entered into your sales order.

 

Ord Date  This is the date you created the order.

 

Inv Date  This is the date you invoiced and completed your sales order.

 

S/Person  You can select the salesperson or user who created this sales invoice.

 

Delivery To  This button allows you to select another address for delivery if this address is different to the billing address.

 

Pick Slip  This button prints out a pick slip for the sales order.

 

Proforma  This button prints out a proforma invoice for the sales order.

 

Refer to the Job Invoicing Chapter for guidance on how to complete the rest of the sales invoice.

 

Choose Invoice or press F12 when the Parts invoice is completed to finalise.

 

If you donwish to finalise the order at this point, click on the Save Button or use the F11 to save your parts invoice.

 

Save  Select Save, if you wish to save the Parts invoice into the Jobs In progress file, which can then be revised, changed and finalised later.

 

Invoice  Select Invoice, if you wish to finalise the Parts invoice.

 

Note: The Payments window appears only if the customer is a cash customer (eg Account terms = 0, as in Job Invoicing).

 

A Print Invoice (with number) window appears. Left click to tick the Details to be included or not to be included in the printed invoice as well as the number of copies you require. Select Go and the report will appear in a report preview window ready for you to print it out through the printer.

 

Note: Tax Invoice is printed in the top left hand corner to indicate that the invoice is a final invoice to be sent to the customer.

 

If a printed invoice is required, print the invoice by left clicking in the printer icon. There is an option to reprint the invoice if you need to and then an option to print out packaging labels, if required.

 

Courier  Use this button to select your courier if you are going to ship the items to the customer.

 

Date  use this date button to choose the date for the items to be picked up.

 

Cartons  indicate the number of cartons that are being shipped.

 

Weight  insert the weight of the package being shipped.

 

Volume  insert the volume of the package being shipped.

 

Time  indicate the time, which the package will be ready for pickup.

 

Consign No  insert the consignment reference number obtained from the courier company when booking the item in for shipping.

 

GST Exempt  Tick this box if you wish to place items on the sales invoice, which will be GST exempt.

 

Disc  shows the amount of discount the customer will receive on this sales invoice.

 

Man  use this tick box and type the amount of the freight manually into the Freight field below.
