CUSTOMER MASTERFILE

 

The Customer Masterfile screen contains the details of all of your regular customers.

When you first install AM-WIN, we suggest that you add your account Customers using this screen. At the time of invoicing, you can also add a customer on the fly.

 

NOTE on colour coding:

Customers with a balance owing will be highlighted in the lookup screen in Blue

Customers with a negative balance will be highlighted in the lookup screen in Red

Suspended customers are highlighted in Grey.

Normal active customers are highlighted in Black.

 

This colour coding is very useful to find customers that have an outstanding balance, especially when receiving payments.

 

 

 

 

 

 

CUSTOMER ACCOUNTS DETAILS

Select the Accounts Details tab in the Customer Masterfile main window, and click on it using the mouse. You can also access this tab using the keyboard by pressing the CTRL + TAB buttons together.

 

Labour Rate  Enter the Labour Rate to be charged for this customer, if applicable. Only enter the labour rate for a customer if it is different to your standard labour rate. You cannot have both a labour Discount and a Labour rate.

 

Note: A specific labour rate for this customer can be set. Regardless of the labour rates on each labour account, the particular labour rate entered, applies to this customer. Only enter a labour rate here if it is different to your standard rate.

 

Stock Price Rate  Enter the Stock Price Rate by clicking the menu and selecting (1-6), if applicable.

 

Note: In the Stock Master file, a particular stock item can have 6 different selling prices (1,2,3,4,5,6). By selecting a Stock Price, at the time of invoicing the customer, a particular set price is selected from the Stocks Master file and applied to this customer.

 

Revenue Discount  Enter percentage allowed for a revenue discount for this customer, if applicable. This discount will then be applied to revenue items on an invoice for this customer.

 

Labour Discount  Enter percentage allowed for labour discount for this customer, if applicable. You cannot have both a labour Discount and a Labour rate. This discount will then be applied to labour items on an invoice for this customer.

 

Parts Discount  Enter percentage allowed for Stock discount for this customer, if applicable. This discount will then be applied to stock items on an invoice for this customer.

 

Invoice Mark up - This option allows you to add a percentage mark up figure which will be added to all prices charged for this customer based on the customers price rate. This means that if you have a price rate of price 1 and a mark up of 10% then the customer will get charged 10% above price 1 automatically. You can also set up one of your price rates as the cost price through the stock multipliers option and use the mark up field to apply a mark up from cost price.

 

Discount Matrix  Enter a set discount for this particular from the menu by selecting [A-J] stock group discount. If not applicable, select No.

 

Note: See the discount matrix section in the Tools Menu section of this manual for how to set the discount matrix up.

 

Print Rec List  Select Yes, No or List from the menu, if a recommended retail list is require to be printed for this customer on an invoice.

 

Defaults  The default customer settings option allows you to configure a range of specific setting for a certain type of customer. These settings can then be grouped so that whenever a new customer is added, the new customer can take on the same attributes as other similar customers. For example your trade customers would have certain discount and price structures, belong to certain groups and territories and have other customer options in common. Rather than manually setting these every time a new customer is added, you can set up a default customer type and have these setting automatically apply to all of these customers.

 

Under the Tools Menu from within AM-Win you are able to select the Default Settings for Customers option. This option then allows you to insert, change or delete a default customer type.

 

To add a new default customer type, click on the insert button or press the INSERT key.

 

You are then prompted to add a Default Group code and description, which can then be used when adding a new customer through the customer masterfiles or when adding a customer on the fly.

 

Enter the information including group, territory, mail group, account terms, price rates, discount rates, labour rates and so on that you wish to use for this default customer type. Refer to the customer masterfiles for clarification on these fields if necessary.

 

You can then change or delete a customer default type using the change or delete keys.

 

The next step is to add a new customer and apply these settings to that customer. From the customer masterfile screen you must first insert a customer in the usual way. After entering the customercode, name and address you can then click on the Defaults button above the job invoice button on the right side of the screen and select the type of customer you are entering. The customer being added will then automatically take on all of the default attributes of that default customer type. This not only saves you time it also means that you are more consistent and efficient with the way your customers are configured.

 

You can also select the default customer type when adding a customer on the fly from the invoicing modules.

 

Location  This option is only available to customers who are licenced for multiple locations. The location button allows you to select the location that this customer belongs to. If you want a customer to be available to all locations then it should be set to location 0. However, if you want to limit a customer only to be available to a particular location then select the location this customer belongs to.

 

Term Limit  Enter term limits by clicking menu and selecting the number of

days (30, 60, 90, 120), if applicable. Password level must be 80+ to override this during an invoice.

 

Note: If the customer exceeds the credit or term limit, an invoice cannot be generated for that customer unless the user holds a password level of 80 plus. Passwords are set in the Control System.

 

Credit Limit  Enter the credit limit allowed to this particular customer, if applicable. You must have a password level of 80+ to override this during an invoice.

 

Monthly Account Fee - Enter a fee to be charged on an account for this customeroverdue account, if applicable. This fee is applied by the user either at the beginning or end of each month by using the Customer account keeping fee option under the Month end options from the Ledger menu. The fee applies to all outstanding balances.

 

Note: Either a fee percent or an account fee can be applied on this particular Customer, it cannot be both.

 

Monthly Fee Percent  Enter the percentage to be charged (liquidated damages) on this customeroverdue account, if applicable. The fee percentage applies to all outstanding balances.

 

Rating - Enter a rating for this particular customer. Eg. AA for good customers and X for bad customers.

 

Offset ledger Code  There are 3 offset accounts to configure. There is the parts offset, which is used to offset parts charges on an invoice, a labour offset and a revenue offset against any revenue items charged. This means that if the invoice has $100 in parts, $60 labour and $20 revenue, the offset accounts will get a negative value corresponding to the amount of each section. For example a -$100 will be put against the parts offset account. This means the actual invoice posting will be zero ($0.00) and no GST will apply.

 

The individual parts, labour and revenue charges will process correctly and be offset in the ledger against the appropriate offset expense accounts such as Vehicle Maintenance etc. If you wish you can offset all sections (Parts, Labour and Revenue) against the same ledger account.

 

Export Customer  Click the box to insert a tick for Yes or click again to remove the tick from the box for No to indicate whether this customer is an overseas customer. If you have ticked the box for YES: GST will not be charged to this customer.

 

Show unalloc Crdts in Paywindow  If this is ticked, then when a supplier or customer credit note is not allocated to an invoice, the credit note is added to the outstanding invoices in customer payments. This occurs when you get a credit note that cannot be allocated or you do not wish to allocate to an outstanding invoice. When the payment is processed you can tick the unallocated (negative) credit note and this will automatically be allocated against the payment you have made without affecting other outstanding invoices. Allocated credit notes are then automatically adjusted against the unallocated balance in either the customer or supplier files. This makes it easier to reconcile statements against your payment transaction.

 

Invoice details include GST if this option is ticked then all of the items on the invoice are printed as inclusive of GST. Otherwise they are printed as ex GST and the GST is added at the bottom of the invoice.

 

Attach batch invoices- If the attach invoices option is ticked then the software will allow you to print a copy of all invoices that were processed for this customer with the statements at the end of the month. If you do not wish to send out an invoice every time you process one for this customer you can simply attach the invoices to the statement at the end of the month. This process can be done from the Batch Invoice printing option under the Reports menu.

 

Use Progressive Invoices- This option allows you to set this customer to default for using progressive invoicing. Progressive invoicing allows you to process more than one invoice against the same job. This means that one job can be invoiced more than once either to the same customer or different customers. If a job needs to be part invoiced to an insurance company, part to warranty and part to the customer then progressive invoicing allows this. You can also use this option to invoice large jobs at the end of each month even if the job hasnbeen completed.

 

Must Have Order Number - The customer masterfiles has a new option that allows you to force the entry of an order number for a customer when an invoice is created. If the order number is not supplied, the system will not allow an invoice to be finalised. However, the invoice can be saved before an order number is required.

 

Opening Balance lock  Enter the opening balance for this customer.

 

Note: An opening balance can only be entered when inserting/creating a new customer, or if no transactions have been done for an existing customer who has a zero balance. To enter opening balances for a customer you need to use the Update Master Balances option under the Tools menu.

 

Print owners address on invoice  this option prints the owneraddress on invoices, which have a separate Bill To in the delivery address area so that the owneraddress is available to do onsite servicing or to deliver goods. Alternatively, if it is not ticked, then only the Bill To address is printed. This needs to be set up on the bill to customer file.

 

Do not print statements  If this option is ticked, then a statement will not be printed for this customer during a normal statement run or through an email statement run. However you can still manually print a single statement for this customer.

 

Default to Docket Printer  if you have a docket printer attached to your computer or somewhere on the network, by ticking this option all receipts and invoices will be printed through the Docket printer.

 

Charge Stamp Duty  this option if ticked will charge stamp duty for all hires charged through the hire system module. Stamp duty only applies in some states and not others.

 

After all the relevant details have been entered for this customer, save the details by choosing Save button located at the top of the CustomerMaster file window.

 

BANK DETAILS

 

The Bank Details Tab allows you to enter the credit card and bank account details that this customer may use to pay their account. These include credit cards, debit cards, cheque accounts and direct deposit details.

 

To insert a new credit card or other account you can press the Insert key or click on the Insert button. A new window will appear for you to enter the account details.

 

Enter the account type from the dropdown list available.

Enter the account or credit card number in the account number field.

Enter the Account name on the card or account in the name field

Enter the Expiry date of the credit card (leave blank for cheque accounts etc)

 

The Method is linked to your payment methodswhich are updated from the Others > Maintenance > Methods menu option. The method is selected by clicking on the button next to the method field.

 

Enter the Bank Name such as Commonwealth Bank West Ryde etc.

 

EFT Code button -

 

You can then press the Enter key or click on the OK button to save the details for the customer. However, the details will not be actually saved to the customer file unless you click the SAVE button at the top of the Customer Masterfile screen as well.

 

You can add additional details by inserting again or change details for the selected item by clicking on the change button or delete the highlighted item by clicking the delete button.
