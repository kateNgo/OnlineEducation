CREATING A CUSTOMER

 

In the AM-WIN window, click the Customer Master Files icon or from the menu choose Customers, left click and choose Masterfile, left click.

 

The Customer's Masterfile Window appears.

Click on the Insert Button in the left hand corner of the screen to insert a new customer. The Insert Code window will appear requiring you to enter a new customer code.

 

Customer Code - Type the first 4 characters of the business name, if the customer is a business or type the first 4 characters of the customer's surname, if it is a personal customer. AM-WIN will automatically insert a numeric number at the end of the code to make it unique, hence you will end up with something that looks like: ABCD01, ABCD02 etc

 

Note: If the Customer name only has 3 characters, type a fullstop (.) after the 3 characters to allow consistency in customer coding.

 

Click OK to confirm or Cancel to abort.

 

 

CUSTOMER DETAILS TAB

 

Use the TAB key or place the cursor in the relevant field by clicking on that field with the mouse to update the customer's details.

 

Bill to  If the customer is to be billed to a different entity, then you can click on the Bill To button and select the other entitity to be billed to. The Select Customers window appears. Left click on the customer to whom bill is to be sent. This highlights the customer to be automatically inserted in the Bill to field, then choose Select. This is useful when multiple branches for the same business are bileld to one head office account. The Bill To customer must have previously been created.

 

Status

Active

The status will automatically be set to 'Active'. This is the default and most common choice. You can then change the statusfor this customer by selecting from the Status drop-down.

 

Suspended

The customer will be suspended from trading and will no longer be displayed in the customer lookup window until they are changed back to 'Active'.

 

Internal

Sometimes you need to invoice parts and labour and other charges to a vehicle or customer who is in fact owned by you. This will occur when you work on your own vehicles or you have your own fleet of vehicles / assets that you maintain. When you charge yourself for the goods and services provided to yourself it seems pointless to have to also pay yourself and then pay GST on your payment, which you then claim back. Sounds complicated? Well it was until now.

 

If you are in the situation above then you should set up a customer who is an internal customer. This customer will be your own company name. In the customer status field change the status to 'Internal'. You are then required to set up the offset ledgers which are used to offset your invoice. There are 3 offset ledgers to be considered and they can be entered from either the Customer Masterfile Accounts tab or the Job Item Masterfiles.

 

The offset settings in the Job Item Master files takes preference over the Customer Masterfile settings. If you manage a fleet of vehicles you may want certain type of vehicles to be offset to different accounts than others. You may want service vehicles offset against to different accounts than spare parts or admin vehicles for example. Or cars offset differently to forklifts.

 

There are 3 offset accounts to configure. There is the parts offset, which is used to offset parts charges on an invoice, a labour offset and a revenue offset against any revenue items charged. This means that if the invoice has $100 in parts, $60 labour and $20 revenue, the offset accounts will get a negative value corresponding to the amount of each section. For example a -$100 will be put against the parts offset account. This means the actual invoice posting will be zero ($0.00) and no GST will apply. The individual parts, labour and revenue charges will process correctly and be offset in the ledger against the appropriate offset expense accounts such as Vehicle Maintenance etc. If you wish you can offset all sections (Parts, Labour and Revenue) against the same ledger account.

 

Third Party

A status of 'Third Party' means that the selected customer is a third party and not the actual customer that the work is being performed for. An example of a third party customer would be an insurance company or a fleet management company whereby work is done for the individual customer but charged to the fleet or insurance company as a separate bill to.

 

The important difference is that you only set this bill to customer as third party if the price structure for invoicing is determined by the customer but paid for by the third party entity. This means if the discount or parts pricing or labour rate is determined by the customer then the bill to would be set to a third party. However, if the discounts and selling prices are set according to the bill to entity then the bill to entity should be set to a status of 'Active' and not 'Third Party'. So if the insurance company were the ones who determined how much you can charge them, then their status would be active and not third party as the owner does not determine the selling prices.

 

Contact

If you set the status to 'Contact', it means that this is not actually a customer but a contact person such as your accountant, doctor, solicitor, friends etc. These customers will not display on the customer lookup screen when processing a payment or an invoice etc. You can make a contact an active customer simply by changing the customer status to 'Active'.

 

Alternate Cust Code  The alternate customer code allows you to provide a customer code to link this customer to the same customer in another system. This will allow data to be exported from AM-Win to the alternate system using the customer code allocated in the other system.

 

Name - Enter the customer's name in full (or personal name if it is a personal customer).

 

Address  Enter the customer's address [Street, Suburb, State, Postcode]. The Suburb, State and Postcode fields can be automatically inserted by Browsing the Postcode file. This is done by left clicking on the find button or pressing the ALT + F keys and then searching for the suburb.

 

Phone  Enter the customer's main contact telephone number. There is no set method of entering phone numbers. You may or may not wish to include the area code and you may or ma not wish to inlude dashes or spaces in the phone number. The most important thing is that regardless of the way you enter details, everyone should do it the same way to make searching more consistent.

 

Mobile Enter the customer's mobile number. This number is primarily used to SMS the customer through the SMS system and also will be printed at the bottom of invoices to enable you to easily contact the customer.

 

Phone 2  Enter the customer's additional contact telephone number if required.

 

Fax  Enter the customer's facsimile number.

 

Terms  Enter number of days or terms for payment of the account. Eg. 7 days, 30 days or C.O.D (or 0 days).

 

Note: if you enter an account terms of 1 day then the system will not display any account terms at all. If a customer is given a term of 1 day then the terms printed on invoices and statements will be suppressed, effectively meaning that no terms are printed. If your terms are set to 30 days, then the system will assume 30 days from end of month. However, any other account terms including 7 or 14 days refer to days from date of invoice.

 

Term Message  Enter  a message which will appear on statements and most invoice formats relative to the customer terms. This message may say something like "From date of invoice" or "From end of month" etc.

 

ABN #  Enter the 11 digit Australian Business number. The system will automatically verify the ABN number is a valid ABN according to a formula provided to us by the Australian Tax Office.

 

Contacts  Enter the name of the customer's contact person. This is used as the contact for overdue letters and other correspondence. Eg, Mrs Brown or Harry. You can also click on the Contacts button to enter multiple contact details for employees who workfor the selected customer. This will allow you to enter the employee's name, Address, Phone number, Email address, etc. You are then able to email invoices and other documents directly to this contact person. You are also able to select one of these contacts as a delivery address to have goods delivered to one of the many delivery addresses you can enter.

 

Third Party ID  The third party ID is a reference field which provides an identification number such as a membership number for this customer. This could be either an NRMA membership number or a Capricorn membership number depending on which information you require for this customer. For more information on the use of this field, please see the Capricorn help section.

 

URL Link   This will allow you to set a link from the masterfile screen directly to either a website or a file on your computer or network or even a folder which contains files relating to the masterfile record. Eg. You may have a substantial number of letters, spreadsheets and other correspondence with a particular customer, which you wish to have easy access to. This may be technical information, sales orientated information or even a string of legal information about a customer including overdue letters or demand letters etc. You can then set a URL link to the folder in your computer that the information is kept in. You then simply click on the URL link button and the folder will open displaying the folder contents. You can then double click on the required document to open it in your associated program such as word, power point or excel.

 

When invoices or statements are emailed to the customer, a copy of the document is saved as a PDF file inside the Customer's URL.

 

Web  Enter customer's internet web site address. When you click on the Web button a link is established which takes you to the customer's web site. By clicking on the button to the left of the Web Site address the system will open up your default web browser with the customers website displayed in the browser address.

 

Email  Enter the email address of your customer's contact person. When you left click on the Email button, a link is established to your email system so you can send e-mail to the customer. By clicking on the button to the left of the email field address the system will open up your default email software with the customer's email in the recipient box ready to send. Furthermore, the first email address is used by default when emailing invoices, receipts, adjustment notes, statements and other documents to the customer through AM-Win.

 

Email 2    Enter any other alternative email address of your customer's contact person.

 

Email 3    Enter any other alternative email address of your customer's contact person.

 

Email 2    Enter any other alternative email address of your customer's contact person.

 

Email Statements   This option allows you to flag a customer to have their statements emailed by default rather than to be printed and sent by mail or fax. If the tick box is ticked then the statement will be created as a PDF file and attached to an email using your default email program. The system uses the first email address from the masterfile but you can change the address if required prior to sending the email. You can also change which email address the statements are sent to from the Control System.

 

Email Document / Invoices   This option allows you to flag a customer for their invoices and other documents to be emailed by default rather than to be printed and sent by mail or fax. If the tick box is ticked then when an invoice or other document is printed, it will create a PDF file and attach that PDF file to an email using your default email program. You can then add comments to the email if required and send the email in the usual way. The system uses the first email address from the masterfile but you can change the address if required prior to sending the email. You can also change which email address the invoices are sent to from the Control System.

 

Group  Customers can be put into relevant customer groups and then be reported on based on each customer group. The customer can be entered into a group by left clicking on the Group button to browse the Customer GROUP File, then clicking on a group and then clicking Select.

 

Mail   This field allows you to add in a mail group which is a group used to dissect your customers for when you wish to send out letters to specific groups of customers such as trade customers etc.

 

Territory  Enter the customer type or description, if applicable or leave blank if not applicable. This allows you to dissect customers into different territories, which will allow different reports to be printed according to a particular type of customer.

 

Contact By   Allows you to enter how you wish to contact a specific customer. Some of the options you might choose could be by ‘SMS’, ‘Email’, ‘Telephone’, ‘Letter’ etc. Before you are able to record the contact method, you must first set up a standard list of contact methods. This can be done under the Attributes menu. You can then insert an attribute with a type ‘Contact Method’.

 

Location  This option is only available to customers who are licenced for multiple locations. The location button allows you to select the location that this customer belongs to. If you want a customer to be available to all locations then it should be set to location 0. However, if you want to limit a customer only to be available to a particular location then select the location this customer belongs to.

 

Salesperson The system allows you to attach a salesperson to a specific customer. This salesperson will then default as the salesperson fot all sales generated for this customer. You are able to change the salesperson for a specific sale if required. You are then able to filter the customers for only customers for a specified salesperson using the 'Sales Filter' button. You are also able to provide statements and other reports specific to a salesperson including only displaying debtors managed by a salesperson from the Debtor Aged Analysis screen.

 

Stock Price Rate  Enter the Stock Price Rate by clicking the drop-down menu and selecting the appropriate price provided to this customer. If a price doesn't exist in the Stock Masterfile under this rate, it will automatically refer to the next rate up in the Stock Masterfile. You canthen apply several discount levels relating to this price.

 

Note: In the Stock Master file, a particular stock item can have 6 different selling prices (1,2,3,4,5,6). By selecting a Stock Price, at the time of invoicing the customer, a particular set price is selected from the Stocks Master file and applied to this customer.

 

Source The Source allows you to nominate how the customer came to be a customer, or how they found out about you. You may select options such as 'Google', 'Word of Mouth', 'Referral', etc. You can then report on the success of your advertising by nominating how the customer came to you and then using the source reports available. You can set up a list of sources from Attributes menu, under Job Source.

 

Sub Source A sub source allows you to break down a source into various sub categories. For instance, you may have a source called “Advertising” with a sub source of “Yellow Pages”, “Google”, “Newspaper”, “Radio” etc. Alternatively, the source may be “Newspaper” and the sub source may be a range of different newspaper types. You can set up a list of sub sources from the Attributes menu, under Sub Source.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Last Invoice, Date and Amount  This field provides the last invoice number and date, issued to this customer. (Automatically updated)

 

Posted next month - the user cannot access this field. It is used to display the value of invoices that were forward posted to next month and will not appear on the customerbalance until the month is closed and rolled over to the next month. See the help on forward and back posting for more details.

 

Notes  Enter any relevant notes regarding this customer. If there are any notes recorded, the Notes icon will be in bold. You can then click on the notes icon and be taken to the notes tab to view the notes.

 

Created Date  The created date is the date that the customer was first entered into the software.

 

Ytd Turnover  This field provides the total sales to this customer for the year. (Automatically updated)

 

Mtd Turnover  This field provides the total sales to this customer for the current month. (Automatically updated)
