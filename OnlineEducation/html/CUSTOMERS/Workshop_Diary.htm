Workshop Diary

WORKSHOP DIARY

 

AM-WIN has a workshop diary where you can view jobs booked in for the day, or extra notes for the selected day. You can also edit a job and look at the available time available for each day. The workshop diary is available under the main AM-Win menu option, Customer>Workshop Diary.

You can set the Workshop Diary to open up automatically when you first open AM-WIN from an option, which is set in the Control System. Go to the Main menu Others>Control System>System Tab and under the General Options tab>Options menu, tick the box which says Workshop Diary on Opening Amwin.

 

When the workshop diary opens the employee availability window will also open over the top of the workshop screen. (Unless this feature has been disabled from the Control System Options tab) This window displays the available employees and the hours per day chargeable for that employee. This information is obtained from the employee file within AM-Win.

 

Double clicking on each employee will mark that employee as in or as away for the day. Once selected, the employee will remain in or away until it is changed. The total value of hours available for the day are then calculated according to the employees who are marked as in for that day. These details can be changed by clicking the Employee button from within the diary screen. This button is to the left of the Print and Insert buttons with an icon of a person on it.

 

By clicking on any of the days on the calendar, you can view any jobs booked in for that day at the bottom of the workshop diary. The jobs displayed are based on any saved jobs which you have done within the Job or Workshop Invoicing options or booked in from the diary. You can also view any notes you have written for that particular day. To write notes for the day, just select the day you want on the calender, and then click into the feather near the blank notepad area on the right hand side and a Note screen will appear. Just type your details into it and click on Save.

 

You can clear the notes by pressing the Clear Note button situated underneath the notepad.

 

Whenever there are jobs booked in for a day, the date will appear in Red, and the number of jobs booked in will appear in brackets at the bottom right corner of the date. Whenever there are notes for that day, but no jobs booked in, the date will appear in blue.

 

At the top right corner of the diary window, the total hours available for the day will be displayed. If jobs have been allocated an estimated time then the available hours will be displayed at the top right hand corner of the date. The available hours are calculated from the total hours less the hours estimated for all jobs booked in for that day. If you change to a new day by clicking on that day with the mouse then ensure that the correct total hours are set by clicking on the employee button. It may be that an apprentice attends TAFE 2 days a week and will not be in tomorrow or the next day. It may also be that you are unaware that a staff member will be on holidays during a certain period.

 

The workshop diary will automatically open displaying todaydate. However, the date can also be accessed by pressing the Today button located at the top of the workshop diary. To view a different month to the current month, click on the buttons which only have one arrow on them. This will move the months forward or backwards. To view a different year, click on the buttons which have two arrows on them. This will move you forward or backwards in years.

 

BOOKING IN A JOB

 

You are able to book in new jobs directly from the Workshop diary!

 

To do this, select and click on the date you want to book in the job for. Then press the Insert button (+ Button) underneath the Notepad section. This will open up an Insert New Job window. To insert a customer, click on the button next to the customer field, and select a customer. Automatically, a Select Customer job item window will appear from which you can choose the job item for the job.

 

Alternatively, you can click on the button next to the Job Item (unless labeled differently) field and select a job item for the job. The customer who owns this job item will automatically be inserted into this window.

 

Then type in an Order number in the order number field. Select the Booking Date or leave it as the current default date.

 

Enter the Estimated hours to complete the job, which is used by the diary to calculate the available hours for the day.

 

Type in or select a Job description from the Description button. The job descriptions can be added from the job description search screen by using the Insert button or changed using the Change button or deleted by using the Delete button. The job description will be automatically set to the default description that you have created in the Control System under the Invoice Tab. If left blank then the system will use a default description of General Work.

 

JOB NUMBER AND STATUS

Within the Insert New Job screen there is a field called Job Number. Usually this field is automatically filled in once you save a job. However you now have the option to manually type your own job number into this field BEFORE you save the invoice, just click into the job number field and type in a job number. This cannot be the same as a previous job number and the job will be saved with this number.

 

Select the Status (or salesperson) by clicking on the status button. A status can be the salesperson looking after this job or the mechanic or a job status such as waiting on parts or waiting for customer approval etc. The salesperson or status can be updated from the salesperson option under the Others > Maintenance>Salesperson File menu in AM-Win. Click the OK button or press the Enter key to select your status. This job will be added to the bottom of the Workshop Diary screen for the day it is booked in for.

 

You can edit or delete these jobs located at the bottom of the Workshop Diary by clicking on the job so that it is highlighted, and then clicking on the edit or Delete button. You can also close the workshop diary by clicking on the Cancel button at the top of the screen.

 

WORKSHOP DIARY BUTTONS:

 

Labour Allocation Button - The workshop diary edit screen now has the Labour Allocation function within the diary screen to enable you to record start time, finished time, time taken for a job and the assigned technician.

 

Invoice button (red toolbox)  You can either double click on a selected job or highlight the job and click on the invoice button. This will open the selected job into the workshop invoicing module so that the job can be updated and/or finalised. See the workshop invoice module for more details.

 

Print Button  Clicking on the print button will provide you with two choices. You can select either a Job Card or a Job Summary.

 

Print Job Card  If you select to print a job card from the print button then you will have the choice to print all jobs for the selected day or just the single job that is currently highlighted at the bottom of the screen or to cancel the selection.

 

Print Summary  If you choose to print the job summary from the print button then you are asked for a date range to print the summary for. The date range will default to the current day. If a larger date range is selected then the report will show all jobs booked in for that date range. Each day will be separated so that the jobs for each day are printed under that day as well as any notes for that day and the total value of work in progress for that day. At the end of the report the total value of all jobs for the date range selected will be printed.

 

Current Button - In the Workshop Diary screen there is a new button called Current. This button allows you to move all of the jobs in progress prior to the current date forward to the current date. All unfinalised jobs will now be moved to today. Beware that this may also bring forward jobs that are unfinalised because they are duplicated or should have been deleted.

 

Customer Button  This button brings up the Customer and Job Item details for the selected job and allows these details to be updated. See the Insert Customer and Job Item help for more details.

 

Refresh Button  This button refreshes the screen will all of the current details. If a job is added, finalised or changed from other areas of AM-Win then it is not automatically updated from an open diary window. The refresh button updates the dairy with any details that have changed since the diary was last opened or refreshed.
