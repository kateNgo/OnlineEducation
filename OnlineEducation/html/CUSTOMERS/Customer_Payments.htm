Customer Payments

CUSTOMER PAYMENTS

 

This option is utilised for when a customer wishes to allocate a payment to a certain outstanding account/s.

 

TO MAKE A PAYMENT:

 

From the AM-WIN menu, click the Customer Payment icon (the 8th icon from the left) or from the menu choose Customers, left click and choose Payments, then click Open item Payments to open up the Customer Payment (Open item) window and to select a customer. You can search for your customer using the Code, Name, Phone tabs. Left click on any of these tabs to search for a customer, then start typing customer information into the locator field and the system automatically starts searching for a match as you type.

 

Alternatively, you can scroll down the customer list and select the customer by left clicking on the customer entry. This highlights the customer who is making the payment. Then choose Select to enter the details and information of the customer and the amounts on any invoices outstanding into the Customer Payments (Open Item) screen. To change a customer while in the Customer Payments (Open Item) screen, left click on the Customer button or press F10 to bring up the customer list again. Then select the customer name.

 

Date  the date is automatically inserted into the date field. To change this date left click on the Date button, which will bring up a calendar window. Use the arrow buttons to scroll through to find the relevant date. To insert a date, double click on the number and it will be automatically inserted in the payments screen.

 

Customer Payment Method  Click on the Method button above the method field and select the method of payment or choose from the menu by left clicking on Others> Maintenance > Method. The Method File window will appear.

 

In the Supplier payments, Supplier credits and cheque entry, when you select the payment method, the lookup table shows Cash, Bank1 etc, This now shows the Method descriptions as set in the GL Masterfile.

 

Left click on the method of payment by customer, choose Select. Alternatively, you can enter details into the locator button and when a match is highlighted, choose Select.

 

If you wish to allocate a payment or credit that has been previously made to currently outstanding invoices then select the Alloc method. See the help on Unallocated payments for more information.

 

Description  Enter a description, eg. Account Payment, July account, Bank Branch etc

 

Cheque/Ref  Enter cheque reference or details, if any. If the payment was a direct deposit then you may wish to enter the bank statement page or some other identifying reference.

 

Amount  Enter the amount received by the customer or to be allocated to the payment.

 

Accept  click on this button to accept the payment details as being correct before entering into the system the amount to be tendered. You can now tag the invoices which will be paid using the money tendered.

 

Tendered - In customer payments you can now have the software calculate the relevant change. This means that if you record a payment of $100 for an $80 invoice then you can enter the cheque for the amount of $100 and the system will allow change in cash to be given to the value of $20.

 

To do this, firstly, you enter the payment amount which is equal to the balance amount of the payment or invoice (ie $80) then you enter the actual cheque amount in the tendered field (ie $100) and press the Tab button on your keyboard. The system will calculate your change of ($20). When the entry is posted the cheque value for $100, the cash change of $20 and the debtor value of $80 is all posted correctly. Furthermore, the deposit screen will show the cheque value as $100 and reduce the cash by $20.

 

DrawerName of the cheque is recorded in the payments and deposits file so that if the drawer of the cheque is different to the customer name that the cheque is paying then you have the correct drawer details on the deposit slip. This often occurs if a company pays a private account or visa versa. When entering a customer payment you have the opportunity to enter the drawer name if it is different.

 

Note: The amount received by the customer is automatically inserted into the Left to Post field.

 

Note: If the total amount outstanding for the customer is paid then the outstanding invoices will automatically be tagged as paid and you simply need to process the payment.

 

Settlement Discounts  You can enter the amount of the percentage of discount you wish to give to this particular customer in the Discount % field. The discount amount is calculated and shown in the Discount Amt field.

 

Settlement Amount  this is the discounted amount, which you will need to collect from your customer.

 

Settlement ledg  If there is a difference between the invoice amount and the discount amount this amount of difference gets posted to a settlement ledger. Set up a Settlement Discounts ledger in the ledger masterfiles in the 9000 series to keep a record of the discounts you give to your customers.

 

Toggle - To make payment of a certain amount on a particular invoice, left click on the invoice and this highlights the invoice for which a payment is to be made, then select Toggle or left (double click) on the particular invoice. This (*$*) the invoice to which payment is to be made.

 

Auto Post - Choose the Auto-Post button, if the customer (or the business) does not require certain payment/s to be made on a particular invoice. The auto-post option will automatically distribute the Amount by making payment from the oldest invoice outstanding to the newest invoice.

 

For example, if a customer has two invoices outstanding one dated 21/05/00 and the other dated 21/06/00, by selecting the auto-post option, the system will clear the amount owing from oldest invoice dated 21/05/00 before clearing the amount owing on the later invoice.

 

When processing a Customer or Supplier payment you are now able to click on the Date field heading from within the payment window and sort the outstanding invoices into date order. This may make it easier to process outstanding invoices for a particular date range. From within the customer and also the supplier payments module you are able to produce a report detailing all of the outstanding invoices for the selected customer or supplier. This can then be used to reconcile the accounts.

 

If the Customer wishes to make payment on a particular invoice, tag the invoice. The full amount received is automatically inserted in the Payable Amount (paying amount) column.

 

If the Customer wishes to distribute the amount to make a payment over more than one invoice, highlight the invoice and select the Change button. The Altering Amount window appears. You can then change the amount to be paid to that particular invoice.

 

Details  If you highlight the invoice on the screen, then you click on this Details button, you are able to access the customer history screen where you can see more details in relation to that invoice, such as invoice descriptions, pricing, job cards, stats etc.

 

Balance Amount  Enter the amount you wish to pay on the first invoice. Eg. $3.57. Select OK. The details will be refreshed on the main screen. In the Left to Post field at the bottom of the scree, you will note there will be the initial amount minus the amount allocated to the first invoice, still left to post to the second invoice. Then left click and highlight the second invoice and select Toggle. The amount left to post will be automatically be inserted in the Payable Amount column.

 

Note: the amount in the Left to Post field should now be at a nil balance ($0.00) as the full amount should have been allocated. However, If you choose not to apply the payment to outstanding invoices or there are no outstanding invoices and the customer will go into a credit balance, then when the payment is Finalised a message will appear advising you that the unallocated payment amount will go into the customers unallocated balance. This can then be allocated to invoices at a later time through the customer payments screen. You can view the Unallocated Help for more information.

 

Once you have tagged and allocated an amount to be paid, to finalise the customer payment, click on the Process button. The ledger accounts will be automatically be updated. A print window will then appear allowing you to print a remittance receipt for the customer. You can also email this remittance directly to the customer if you have a modem installed. When a customer receipt is printed the transaction number for that receipt is also printed on the receipt to represent a receipt number. Because the transaction number is used which references back to the payment, the next receipt printed may not be the next transaction number as other transactions such as invoices or journal entries may have been processed between payments being processed.

 

To clear the Customer Payments window, click on the Clear button.

 

To make an enquiry on a customer, supplier or general ledger, click on the Enquiry button.

 

To print out an outstanding balance report for that customer, click on the Outstanding Button.
