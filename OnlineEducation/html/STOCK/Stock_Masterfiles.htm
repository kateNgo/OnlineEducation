Stock Masterfiles

STOCK MASTERFILES

 

 The Stock Master file option is used to enter all your stocked items. Stock items can also be added at the time of purchasing or invoicing, if the stock item does not already exist on file.

 

  To locate a stock Item

 

You can locate a stock item in a number of ways. If you know the stock code or part of it then Left Click on the list of stock code on the left side of the masterfiles window so that one of the stock codes is highlighted (usually in blue). Then simply type on the keyboard the Customer Code or part of it and the system will automatically scroll to and locate the customer.

 

 If you are unable to locate the stock item by stock code then you can click on the Find Button in the Stock Masterfiles window, to search for a particular Stock Item. The Select Stock or Alternate Stock window appears allowing to locate stock in many ways including the intellisearch option. To find out more, click on the Select Stock or Alternate Stock text.

 

NOTE on colour coding:

Serialised Stock Items are highlighted in the lookup screen in Red

Negative Stock on hand Items are highlighted in the lookup screen in Green

Positive Stock on hand Items are highlighted in the lookup screen in Blue

 

 Create or add a stock item file

 

 

In the AM-WIN window, click the Stock Masterfiles icon (4th icon from the left).

The Stock Masterfile window appears.

 

Choose Insert from the top of the Stock Masterfile window.

Stock class.  The stock class options allows you to select a item as Stock, Non Stock, Consignment , Revenue or Trade-in.

  1. 1. Stock: this is a normal stock item and will be treated as such by AM-WIN. This means that it is purchased as an asset into the stock file and full stock control features are available for this item.

 

  1. 2. Non Stock class: a non stock item still allows you to have stock control. However it doesnform part of your stock asset and is treated like any Buy in & sell out revenue item such as 6030 & 7030 revenue accounts. The only difference between revenue and non stock items is that being a non stock item means you can track the purchase & sales details by the part number and not just a revenue account number. This allows better tracking of non stocked items. You should set up your revenue accounts for non-stock items between 6030  6999 and expense accounts between 7030  7999. Non stock items include stock that you may buy and sell on a casual or even regular basis that you donactually keep in stock.

 

  1. 3. Consignment stock class: Consignment stock allows you to track stock which is on consignment from a supplier, such as batteries and tyres. When purchasing consignment stock it is treated just like a non stock item with the only difference being that a consignment stock report can be generated to advise what stock has been sold and needs to be replaced by the supplier. You should set up different revenue and expense accounts for non stock items compared to consignment stock items. You should set up your revenue accounts for Consignment stock items between 6030  6999 and expense accounts between 7030  7999.

 

  1. 4. Revenue class: Revenue stock is also very similar to non-stocked items however the stock on hand is not controlled. This is simply an income item for non-stocked items which are charged out but never actually purchased in as a non-stock item. Although we do not recommend it, labour would be a revenue item as you do not buy labour but you do sell it. Selling a wheel balance or alignment that you do in your workshop would also be classed as a revenue item rather than a non-stock item. You should set up your revenue accounts for Revenue items between 6030  6999 or 4000  4999 or 8000  8999. However, there are no set limitations for these accounts. BE CAREFUL! There are also no expense accounts for these items.

 

  1. 5. Trade in Stock: Trade in stock allows for the correct calculation of GST and the correct processing of stock which is treated as a trade-in under the GST second hand goods legislation. This means that you can only claim a input credit for the price paid for a trade in Item from a private customer at the time that item is sold at a later date. However, if you trade in an item from a business which is registered for GST then you can claim the input credit immediately from the tax office by obtaining a TAX INVOICE for the trade-in from the business customer. This means that you will ONLY set a stock item to trade-in if that particular item is traded from a private customer who is not registered for GST or does not have an ABN number. Otherwise it should be treated as a normal stock item.

 

Example: Trading in a vehicle:

 

Lets say that you sell a car for $22,000 including GST and that car has a part number of ABC123 (usually the rego or stock number of that vehicle).

 

We are then trading in a car XYZ123 for a value of $5500 with no GST as the customer is a private person and not registered for GST and has no ABN number.

 

Firstly we invoice the customer $22000 inc GST for the ABC123 vehicle with a GST content of $2000. We then add a new stock item for the value of $5500 ex GST and $5500 including GST. Even though GST does not apply to this vehicle at this stage still enter it this way and the system will handle it correctly as long as you set the stock class of this item to Trade-In.

 

When the item has been added put it on the invoice as a quantity of 1 (thatminus (-) 1) It will not work correctly if you enter the quantity as a plus.

 

You will note that even though the invoice now totals $16500 the GST is still set to $2000 as the GST is charged on the full sales price and not on the price less the trade-in. This will place a sale price of $22000 in GST for your BAS with a GST component of $2000 and also place a negative NO GST sale price of -$5500 with no GST. The traded item will be updated in the stock file as being a purchase rather than a negative sale.

 

At a later stage you may then sell the traded XYZ123 item. In order to do this you simply invoice the trade in item as a part number XYZ123 as you would sell any other item and you may also trade in a vehicle as described above on that invoice. When selling the XYZ123 item you must sell that item as a positive (+) quantiy of 1. The system will automatically recognise this as a traded item and charge GST on that item.

 

Lets say you sell the Traded Item XYZ123 for $8800 including GST. The invoice will have a total of $8800 (less any further trade-in) with a GST component of $800. The system because it recognises the sale as a trade-in item will also create a GST input entry in the GST purchases file of $5500 with a GST input credit amount of $500 which of course is 1/11th of the traded price. It will then change the ex gst price of the traded item from $5500 to $5000.

 

Description  Enter description of stock which will appear on the invoice.

 

G.S.T.  If the stock attracts the Goods and Services tax (GST), left click and select Yes from the menu. If it does not attract the GST, select No.

 

Ex GST  Enter price of the stock before the imposition of the GST.

 

Inc GST  Enter price of the stock including imposition of the GST.

 

Note : If the GST price of the stock is not known, enter the price excluding the

 GST and the GST price will be automatically calculated and inserted.

  If only the GST price is known, enter the GST price and the price before the

 GST will be automatically calculated and inserted.

 

Average Cost  This field the average value ex gst of the cost of this stock item. For example if you purchased 1 of this item at $1 and 1 of this item at $2 ex gst then you would have 2 in stock at an average cost of $1.50. The average cost is used to calculate the cost of sales when stock is sold and is automatically updated when doing a purchase or purchase order. You cannot manually adjust the average cost.

 

NOTE: The average cost is automatically reset to the last cost ex gst when the stock on hand is zero and a new purchase is made.

 

Bin  Enter the location of the stock number eg. Bin would mean that this part is stored in the row and is in the 12th bin.

 

Pref Sup  Enter preferred supplier of stock. Click on the button on the right of the preferred supplier field. The Suppliers window appears.

 

After you have searched for the supplier in the Suppliers window, this should highlight the supplier which will be automatically inserted into the preferred supplier field. Choose Select to confirm.

 

Note: the preferred supplier is also automatically updated from the purchase orders and purchase credits sections.

 

GROUP - Enter the group the stock belongs to by browsing the Stock Group File.

 

Sub_Group - Enter Sub-Group the stock belongs to, if any.

 

X  Reference  Enter a cross reference for the stock to locate the stocks that are the same but with a different part number or a different supplier.

 

Status  Enter status of stock item. Left click on menu and select Active or Superseded.

 

 Notes : Activemeans that the item is a Stock item

 means that the item has been superseded by another item.

 

Superseded by  the item that has superseded the stock item.

 

Automatic  Select Yes or No from the menu.

 

Note : When an item is set to be superseded by another item, and the quantity on hand has dropped to zero, the auto selection mechanism will return or dial up the part number held in the superseded by field (see step  19).

 

Unit Label  Enter a description for the stock item or click on the menu and select from the list.

 

Prices  The system allows you to have up to 6 selling prices for each item. Usually the prices range from highest to lowest with price 1 being the highest price. The names of these fields can be changed to suit your needs. For example price 1 can be named as retail price 2 as trade etc. These field labels can be changed in the modify system field labels option under the Tools Menu of AM-Win.

Rec  Enter recommended retail price for stock item.

 

Supplier List  Enter supplier list price for stock item usually as list ex gst.

 

Maintain Level  Enter quantity of stock item to be maintained for stock on hand.

 

Lead Time  Enter number of days it normally takes to receive an order of stock item.

 

On order  Enter quantity of stock item currently on order.

 

Not invoiced  Enter quantity of stock item that has been delivered, which has been added to the quantity on hand but has not yet been invoiced from the supplier.

 

Notes on Stock Item  This field indicates whether or not notes exist in the statistics tab for this stock item.

 

Alternate Stock Available  This field indicates whether or not alternate stock has been setup for this stock item.

 

Sell By Volume  This allows you to sell the stock item by volume. If this box is ticked then you are required to enter the total $ value of a sale and the software will calculate the qty based on the unit price. Usually you would enter the qty and the system would calculate the total price. This is used for items such as petrol, liquids etc.

 

Total Stock  Enter total value of stock item.

 

GL Codes - Enter general ledger codes to create Asset, Revenue, Expense, Discrepancy accounts to individual stock items.

Note : The statistics regarding Sales Figures, Purchases and Other details are automatically updated by the transaction processing mechanism when the stock item has been bought or sold.

Alt Stock Enquiries button. - Under the Alternate Stock Button there is also a Alternate stock sales enquiry button which when clicked will allow you to view all sales for the selected alternate stock item.

 

  1. 1. Under the Stock menu there is an option called Alternate Stk Enquiry. When you click on this option a window appears: This window allows you to do a sales enquiry for alternate stock items.

 

  1. 2. There are 2 ways in which you can search for the sales information. You can either search by looking up the Alternate Stock code by clicking on the button next to the field Alternate Stk Code. The Browse the Alternate Stock window will appear for you to choose the stock code.

 

  1. 3. Alternatively, you can search for the Alternate stock by looking up a Stock Code by clicking on the button next to the Stock code field. The Select Stock or Alternate Stock window will pop up. Find the stock item whose Alternate Stock you want to enquire about. Once you choose that stock item the sales information for its alternate stock will appear on your screen.

ALLOCATION TAB

 

Within the stock masterfiles, a new tab has been added called  Stock Allocation. This window is a useful summary displaying all the jobs and manufactured items which this stock item is allocated for.

 

It also displays the quantity it is allocated to and the customer the job or manufactured item is for.

 

At the bottom of the tab window you can view the amount of stock on hand, and the available stock left once that item has been allocated to jobs and manufactured items.

 

In the stock Masterfiles screen under the Stock > Masterfiles screen if you click on the Allocations tab you can view any purchase orders that are currently outstanding for the selected stock item.

 

Choose Delete to delete a stock item in the Stock Masterfile.

 

Note : Whilst a stock exists, it can not be deleted.

 

Choose Clear, to clear the screen (only when creating/inserting a stock item file).
