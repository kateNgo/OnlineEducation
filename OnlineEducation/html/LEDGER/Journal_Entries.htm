Journal Entries

 

JOURNAL ENTRY

 

Important notes about Journal Entries:

The order you enter the journals in does not matter. You do not have to do a debit, then a credit, then a debit, then a credit. Any order or pattern is fine.

 As long as the final balance is zero, you may process the journal entries.

Debit is a PLUS  will increase the value of the account

Credit is a MINUS  will decrease the value of the account

 

Journal entry can be accessed by either left clicking on the Journal Entry icon (the 15th icon), or choosing Journal Entry from the Ledger menu.

 

The Journal Entries window will appear.

 

Back Posting to Last Year or a previous period.

 

You are able to back post transactions to any previous month within the current financial year or back to the previous financial year. This is date dependant. If you enter a date on or before the last day of the previous financial year, the system will display a tick box with a option to post back to the previous year. If you leave this box ticked then the Journal Entry will be posted back to the previous financial year and the current year opening balances will be adjusted.

 

If the last year entry involves an expense or income account then the carried forward balance of the 3030 unappropriated profit and loss account will be updated with the balance of the income and expense items. However, the previous year balances will be updated for the actual income and expense accounts used in the journal.

 

Inserting A New Journal.

 

Left click the insert button.

 

Left click on the button to the right of the GL Code field. This will bring up the General Ledger code window. You may search for the GL code you want and select it by left clicking the Select button.

 

The GL code will then appear in the journal entry window, and the date you are entering the journal will be automatically inserted in the Date field. You may change the date by typing into the date field.

 

Left click (or TAB across from the date field) in the Reference, Description and Amount fields and enter in the appropriate details.

 

Left click on the down arrow next to the Debit/Credit field and choose from Debit or Credit.

 

The Gl Balance field will display the new value of the selected ledger account once the journal has been processed. This is handy to ensure that you have correctly selected the debt or credit option to achieve the desired balance.

 

NOTE: If more than one entry for the same ledger account is processed on the same journal entry then the GL balance only takes into account the currently typed entry and not entries that have already been placed in the list of entries to be processed.

 

Left click the Update button. The journal you have just entered will be displayed.

 

By highlighting any of the journal entries (by clicking on them) you can view itGL name and GL balance, at the bottom of the screen.

 

When any of the entries are highlighted you may also change itdate, reference, description, amount and debit/credit fields by typing into them and then pressing the Update button. You cannot change itGL code.

 

To delete a journal entry from the list to be processed, highlight the entry and left click the Delete button.

 

Once the Left to Post field equals zero you can then left click the Process button at the top of the screen. Once processed a screen will appear giving you the option to enter Journal notes against this entry. These are important so that you can remember the purpose for doing the journal at some later date. The journal notes can be viewed from the Ledger Enquiries screen under the F9 Trans button option. After typing the Journal Notes and clicking on the close button the screen will be cleared and ready for the next entry.

 

Left click the Clear button to clear the screen without processing your entries.

 

Left click the Close button to close the window without saving entries (unless you have already pressed the Process button, in which case journals you just entered would still be saved).
