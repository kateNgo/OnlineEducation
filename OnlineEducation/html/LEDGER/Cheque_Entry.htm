Cheque Entry

 

CHEQUE ENTRIES

 

 

This option allows cheque entries to be made from Bank 1 or Bank 2 or a cash cheque and posted to general ledger expense accounts.

 

To make a cheque entry:

 

In the AM-WIN window, click the Cheque entries icon (the 14th icon from the left) or from the menu choose Ledger, left click and choose Cheque Entry, left click.

 

The Cheque Entry window appears.

 

Date  Enter date of cheque (the current date should be automatically inserted by default).

 

BACK POSTING

 

You may also under certain circumstances back post a cheque entry to a previous month or even to a previous year or GST period. This is done by changing the date back to a date within the previous month. See the BACK POSTING notes for more information.

 

Method  Select by left clicking on the menu where the cheque is from -Cash, Bank 1, Bank 2 etc. The list are of all accounts ranging between 1010 and 1029.

Type- Left click on the down arrow and choose from the types of accounts. For information about the types of accounts, see the following section on Standing Journals.

 

Ledger- enter the Ledger account you wish to post the entry to by left clicking on the button to the right of the Ledger field. The Select Expenses Ledger window appears.

 

Enter Ledger Account to locate it, or select the ledger by left clicking on the ledger name/code. This highlights the general ledger account for which the cheque is being posted. Choose Select.

 

Cheque # - Enter cheque number. If this entry is not for a cheque but for a cash payment or a direct deposit then you may use the bank statement page or some other reference for this entry. It is best to always use a unique reference as it may confuse the system into thinking that the same reference means that this entry is a duplicate of or addition to previous cheque entry.

 

Description  Enter a description of the payment. Eg. Payment of repairs.

 

Amount  Enter amount for the cheque inclusive of any GST.

 

Name  This field will display the ledger account that the cheque will be posted to (as selected in Step 6). This field will be Automatically filled in when the ledger account is selected.

 

Current  (automatically inserted) This is the current month balance of the selected ledger

 

Balance  (automatically inserted) This is the current total balance of the selected ledger

 

Choose Process to post the cheque entry to the relevant journal.

 

Cheque Dissection

 

NOTE: If you wish to dissect a cheque into several different ledger accounts then you may do so as follows.

 

Left click on the box next to Dissection so that a tick appears in it (located on the right side of the screen). When this is done, a new section of the window will added to the bottom of the window.

 

Left click the Insert button or hit the Insert key on your keyboard, to choose a ledger account to allocate some of the total cheque amount to. The Insert Mode window will appear.

 

Left click on the button next to the Expenses Code field and select an account from the Select Expenses Ledger window which will appear.

 

The Expense Desc. field will automatically be filled in when the Expenses Code is selected.

 

The GST Types field will be automatically field in once you have chosen the Expense Account according to the settings for that account. If you wish to change this type left click on the arrow next to the GST Types field and choose from either GST, No GST, Capital Acquisition, Motor Vehicle:

 

Type in the amount you want to allocate to that ledger account in the Expense Amount field.

 

The Dissection window will appear at the bottom of the Cheque Entry window.

 

Left click on the OK button. This will add this entry into the lower section of the Cheque Entry window.

 

Note that the Left to Post field at the bottom illustrates the amount you have left to allocate. The total field displays the amount which you have already allocated.Once you have completed allocating all money, the Left to Post field should equal zero, and the Total field should equal the amount of the cheque.

 

Click on the Insert button to continue to allocate to more ledger accounts, and repeat the above steps.

 

At any time you may change or delete an entry by highlighting it and clicking on the Change or Delete button.

 

Once you have completed allocating the cheque, left click the Process button at the top of the cheque entry screen.

 

Enquiry Button.

 

Clicking on the enquiry button will opening the ledger enquiry window and display the enquiry screen for the selected expense ledger for the current month.
