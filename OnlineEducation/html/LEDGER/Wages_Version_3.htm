Wage Entry

 

AM-WIN now gives you the option to enter gross wages, payg tax, superannunation, additions, deductions and net wages against employee file. This will track and calculate all wages figures required for the BAS statement and provide details required for your End of Year group certificates. The system will provide figures for total wages on a weekly, monthly, 3 monthly and yearly basis for each employee separately and jointly. This is not a payroll system and will not calculate the tax for each employee. However it will allow all of this information to be recorded and an audit trail printed as well as a group certificate for the end of year. This will link to the employees file, and process all appropriate journals automatically, including the Bank Rec and BAS records. There is no need for a manual journal entry to be done.

Your employees need to be set up in the Employee File which is located under the Ledger menu. Please see the Employee File of the full manual for details on setting up this file. Within the Employee File you will be available to view wage details, but only after they have been entered in the wage entry section.

 

Please make sure you have an account set up for Superannunation and Installment Income. The superannuation account should be set up as 2041 and the Installment income set up as 2998. If these accounts to not exist then please create them through the Ledger Masterfiles.

 

To enter wage entries, go to the Ledger menu and choose Wage Entry. The Employees Wage Entry window will open up.

 

Todaydate will be showing in the date field, however this can be changed by clicking on the button next to the date field.

 

Method: Click on the arrow next to the Method field, and choose from which account the wages are being paid from. Please note: if you are processing different wages by different methods, you will have to process the wages separately, in groups of the method they are being paid with. Eg: you may process 6 wages using method bank 1. And then start a new wage entry and process 3 wages using method bank 4.

Description: In this description field type any description you deem appropriate eg: 1st Week April Wages.

Refer/Cheque: type in a cheque or reference number

Amount: Type in the total net amount for all wages you are going to pay. This is the amount after tax and deductions have been deducted and additions have been added.

 

Click on the Insert button and choose an Employee from the employees file window which will appear.

 

This employees details will now be displayed in the Wage window. You can change the employee you have chosen by clicking on the button next to the employee ID field and reselecting.

 

Fill in the appropriate amounts in the Gross Wage, Additions, Tax and Deduction fields. Please note that the gross wage field is the only compulsory field of these four. The Net Wages field and the Super field (superannuation = 9% of gross wage + additions) will be automatically filled in once the other fields have been filled in.

 

Click the Update button. The information will then be added to the bottom of the screen. Please notice the Left to Post field which displays the amount left to allocate to an employee and updates each time you press the update button to enter a wage entry.

You may now click the Insert button, select another employee, and enter in their wage amounts in the gross wage, additions, tax and deduction fields.

 

By clicking on (highlighting) the entries at the bottom of the screen, you may change the amount details (in the gross wage, additions, tax, deductions fields) and then press the update button again to save the changes.

 

Once all wages have been entered, click the Process button to process the wages. please note, the total amount of wages being processed must equal the total amount entered in the amount field located at the top right hand corner of the screen. (ie: left to post = 0).

 

Once process is completed it will state process completed on the wage screen. To begin a whole new wage entry, click on the button next to the date field at the top of the window and select the date, and then continue on repeating all the above steps.

 

Reversing a wage entry.

 

To reverse a wage entry you must enter the details again exactly as you did previously but this time as a negative for all values. EG. -$3000 cheque and  (negative) amounts for all wage, tax and other details. Then process the reversal entry.
