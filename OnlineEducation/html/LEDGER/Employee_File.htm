Employee File

EMPLOYEE FILE

 

This employee file allows you store information regarding your staff members, such as their address, contact details, license number, tax file number etc.

 

Go to the Others menu and select Employees.

 

The Browse the Employee File window will appear (this will be empty when you first open it).

 

To Insert an employee, click on the Insert button. A Record will be Added window will appear.

 

Employee ID: This must be a number and is used in the Browse the Employee File window to call up the employees records.

 

Type in the Employees Name, Address, Initials, Date of Birth, and Phone number.

 

Left click the button next to the City field for Browsing the Postcode file From here you can choose the suburb you wish, and this will automatically insert this suburb into the City field, as well as filling in the State and Postcode.

 

Sex: left click on the down arrow next to this field and choose from M and F.

 

Dependants: left click on the down or up arrows to indicate how many dependants that employee has.

 

M.Status: left click on the down arrow and choose from either Single, Married, Divorced or Widowed.

 

Click on the Employment and Emergency Contact tab.

 

Tax File Number: Enter the employees tax file number. Please note, this number must not be entered in with any spaces between the digits. The system will automatically separate the numbers once you tab out of this field.

 

M.V. Licence: Enter the employees drivers licence number.

 

Trade Licence: Enter the employees trade licence number.

 

Type in the Job Title and Dept. Name of the department the employee is working in.

 

Date Hired: Click on the button next to the Date Hired field. A calender will pop up. Using the arrows on the calender find the correct year and month the employee was hired, and then click on the exact date on the actual calender. (Please see the using the calender section of the manual if you have any problems).

 

Status: click on the down arrow next to the Status field and choose from Full time, Part time, or casual.

 

Pay Period: click on the down arrow next to the Pay Period field and choose from weekly, fortnightly or monthly.

 

Name (next of Kin/Emergency): type in the name of the employees next of kin or emergency contact.

 

Phone: type in the phone number for the employees emergency contact.

 

Employee and Wage Details Tab.

 

You can also add in the default wage amounts for each employee including the Gross Wage, Tax, Additions, Deductions and super. These defaults will flow through to the wage entry system as default values.

 

Tax File Number. Enter the employees 9 digit tax file number.

 

Pay Period. Enter the pay period as either weekly, fortnightly or monthly. Default is weekly.

 

Motor Vehicle Licence Enter the employees Drivers Licence Number.

 

Expiry Date Enter the expiry date of the drivers licence.

 

Trade Licence # Enter the employees Trade Licence Number.

 

Expire Date Enter the expiry date of the Trade licence.

 

Hours / Day This field holds the estimated hours per day that this employee is productive for as a tradesperson. If this employee does not perform chargeable hours of work then do not enter a value in this field. If this employee is an apprentice then it may be that they are productive for 4 - 5 hours per day instead of the 7.6 hours that they actually work. This field is used in the workshop diary to calculate the available hours left in a day base don the estimated time to complete each job

 

Job Title Enter the job title such as Mechanic, Manager, Secretary etc.

 

Department Name The department name is used to separate areas of the business into different categories such as Workshop staff, office staff, spare parts staff etc.

 

Date Hired This is the date that this employee was first hired.

 

Status This is the status of the employee such as Active or Terminated.

 

Termination Date This is the date that the employee was terminated. Terminated employees do not appear on the list to receive wages.

 

Wage A /C Enter an account from the ledger to use to record the wages for this employee. Typically a workshop staff member would have an account in the 5000 account series, a sales / parts employee in the 7000 series and a admin staff member in the 9000 series accounts. See the ledger setup area for more information. Accounts can be added through the ledger masterfiles.

 

Tax A/C Enter an account from the ledger to use to record the tax for this employee. Typically a workshop staff member would have an account in the 5000 account series, a sales / parts employee in the 7000 series and a admin staff member in the 9000 series accounts. See the ledger setup area for more information. Accounts can be added through the ledger masterfiles. The wage and tax account can be the same. The wage report will separate these into different values.

 

 

Super A/C Enter an account from the ledger to use to record the superannuation for this employee. Typically a workshop staff member would have an account in the 5000 account series, a sales / parts employee in the 7000 series and a admin staff member in the 9000 series accounts. See the ledger setup area for more information. Accounts can be added through the ledger masterfiles. The wage, tax and superannuation accounts can be the same. The wage report will separate these into different values.

 

 

Usual Gross Wage Enter the usual Gross wage for this employee. This can be changed at the time of doing the wages.

 

Usual Addition Enter the usual Additions for this employee. This can be changed at the time of doing the wages. Additions include meal allowances, car allowances etc.

 

Usual Super Enter the usual Suerannuation for this employee. If left blank then the system will calculate the super as 9% o fthe gross wage and additions. This can be changed at the time of doing the wages and should be changed if the staff member pays additional super as an employee salary sacrifice.

 

Usual Deduction Enter the usual Deductions for this employee. This can be changed at the time of doing the wages. Deductions include union fees, child maintenance etc.

 

Usual Tax Enter the usual Tax for this employee. This can be changed at the time of doing the wages.

 

Click on the big green tick to save this record, or the big red cross to close the window without saving it.

 

Remarks Tab

 

Click on the Remarks tab. This will open up the notes window where you can write any information you wish to include on this employee. These remarks will appear in the Browse the Employee file window.

 

All this information will now be displayed in the Browse the Employee File window.

 

To Change any information, left click on the change button and change any details in the Record will be changed window which will come up.

 

To Delete any record, highlight the record in the left hand column, and then click the delete button.

 

Please note: the columns on the left hand side which has a list of all employees can be sorted by employee ID or by last name, depending on which tab you click on at the top of the Browse the Employee File window.

 

Viewing wages in Employee File

 

Once wage entries have been processed, they can be viewed under the Employee File which is located under the Ledger menu.

On the left hand side of the Employee File, click on the employee whose wage details you would like to view. On the right hand side of the employee file, click on the Wages/Salary tab.

 

This new screen you will see, displays at the top the ledger accounts that Wages, Tax and Superannuation go into. These ledger codes are set up within the Employee file when you first set the employee up. You can change them by clicking on the employees name and clicking the change button at the top of the employee File window. Then look under the employment & emergency contact tab in the record will be changed window which will appear.

 

In the Wages/Salary window, as you click on different months you will view different amounts under Month to Date Account for (month youselected) title. These amounts cannot be changed.

 

In the bottom part of the screen you can also view the details for each month, such as the gross wages, tax, deductions, additions and net wages. Each time you process wages for this employee in a particular month, the figures are added on to the previous figures existing in that month. Therefore, if you process $200 net wages for Mr ABC 4 times a during the month of April, at the end of April this window should show that his total wages is $800 for April.

 

At the very bottom of this window, you can view the Year To Date wage figures for this employee in the YTD Totals Fields

 

Printing Wage Report

 

While you have an employee selected you may print a wage report for this employee (or for all employees) by clicking on the Report button at the top of the Employee File.

The wages report window opens. Within this window you can choose whether you want to print a report for all employees, or just the single employee you have selected.

 

If you choose single employee, but have changed your mind about the employee you want to print, simply click on the button next to the employee ID field in this window and select the employee you want.

 

Within the From and To fields, you can click on the arrow and select the month range you wish to view. If you wish to view just for the month of May, you would make both these fields say May.

 

If you want a report for the whole financial year so far, you would choose June in the From field, and the current month in the To field. Then press the Go button to view and/or print the report.

 

Please note: this wage report can also be printed from the Reports -> Accounting menu under Wage Report.

Licence Expiry Date in Employee File

 

  1. 1. 1. Within the employee file you can now enter an licence expiry date, or any date (such as an employees birthday) that you want to be reminded of 3 days beforehand.

  1. 2. 2. Within the Employee File  when you are first setting the employee up, there is a field called Expiry Date under the Employment & Emergency Contact tab. This is where you type in the date you want to be notified of.

 

  1. 3. 3. If you have already set up the employee but forgot to enter the expiry date, highlight the employee in the employee file, and click the change button. You can then go into the Employment /Emergency Contact tab and enter in the expiry date.

 

  1. 4. 4. When it is 3 days before the expiry date, AM-WIN will give you a message when you open it stating whose expiry date is due to expire, and the exact date. This message will pop up each time you open AM-WIN from 3 days before the expiry date, until after the expiry date.
