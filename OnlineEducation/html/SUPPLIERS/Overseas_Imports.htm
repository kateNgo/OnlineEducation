Overseas Imports

 

IMPORTING STOCK FROM OVERSEAS

 

When importing Stock from an overseas supplier you will later receive an invoice from customs and from a freight company relating to the customs, GST and freight which applies to the delivery of the parts into Australia. In purchase orders a tick box can be selected to allocate the GST, freight, customs etc to a previous parts invoice. The additional charges are then proportioned to the invoice from the supplier of the parts and as a result the cost of the parts are increased.

 

Go to the Suppliers menu and choose Purchase Orders.

 

Do the Purchase Order as usual.

 

The Proportion Cost tick box will only become active if only Revenue is added to the Purchase Order. If there are parts and revenue on the purchase order you cannot proportion the cost.

 

The Proportion Cost tick box is situated under the Status/Sales field at the top of the screen.

 

Click on the Proportion Cost tick box in order to tick it.

 

Click on the save button, and then the Final button in order to finalise the Purchase Order

 

Type in the Reference number, and then click on the Final button again.

 

The Proportion Cost over Invoices window will appear.

 

Click on the Insert button.

 

Then click on the button next to the Invoice field.

 

A Select Purchase window will appear with the dates of invoices and the amounts.

 

Select the invoice you want to proportion the Purchase Order to, by highlighting it and clicking the Select button.

 

The details of this invoice will now appear in the Date and amount fields in the Proportion Cost over Invoices window.

 

Click on the OK button. The invoice should now appear in the Proportion Cost over Invoices window.

 

Click on the Complete button to process the Purchase Order.
