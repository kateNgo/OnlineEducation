Amex / Diners Payments

 

Cards such as American express, Diners and others deduct fees from the payment prior to the money arriving in your bank account. When the card payment is made the customer has paid, but the money may not arrive in your account for several days. When it does, its not for the full amount, but an amount less a percentage fees which the card provider withholds.

 

So how do we process these payments?

 

Setting Up

 

Firstly setup a withholding account to hold the payment until it arrives in your bank account. This holding account should be an account range between 1011 and 1019 (the payment accounts). Lets called it 1015 for now. This account would be setup using the General Ledger Masterfiles. You must also have a card fees account setup in the 9000 series account. Say 9150 Card Fees.

 

Receiving Payment

 

Now we must go to the payment methods area under the Others > Maintenance menu and create a payment method called Amex or Diner or another appropriate name for the card. You then link the 1015 account to the new payment method as detailed in the payment methods area.

 

Now when the customer pays their account using the card, you are able to receive the payment using the new payment method you have created. Payment details can be found in the Customer Payment or the Job Invoice Payment help.

 

Banking the money

 

The payment now sits in the 1015 (or other account 1011  1019) ready for it to arrive in your bank account. When the money the arrives in your account, go to the Journal Entry Screen and journal the amount actually received from 1015 to 1020 (or your alternative bank account). To do this we Credit 1015 and Debit 1020. This leaves the card fee still in the 1015 account.

 

NOTE: Do not Journal the card fees from the 1015 account as they have a GST component which will not be accounted for when doing a journal.

 

Processing the fees

 

To process the fees, go to the cheque entry screen. Select the 1015 account as the Method of payment and then select the Amex / Diners fees account (in the 9000 admin expense series) as the expense account. Enter the amount of the fee including GST. The GST will automatically be calculated. This will transfer the fee to the fees account, out of the 1015 account and at the same time account for the GST.

 

 

Now for the AM-Win EASY WAY.

 

Go into the Deposits screen. Select the bank account associated to the card charges (1015). Tag the records to be processed. Select the bank account to deposit the money in. Now at the bottom of the screen, enter the amount of the card fees and then select the ledger account to process the fees into. Process the deposit and the fees will be automatically accounted for including the GST component and the bank account will get the deposit amount less the fees.
