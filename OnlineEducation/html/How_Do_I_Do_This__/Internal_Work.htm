Internal Work

 

Internal work such as fleet / asset management, work on your own vehicles, work making tools for internal use and even cleaning the workshop floors can be accounted for as an internal invoice.

 

There are two ways to process an internal invoice.

 

1. Using the Offset Accounts as detailed in the version 6 help.

 

2. Processing negative charges on an invoice.

 

The 2nd option means that you charge the labour, parts and revenue items to an invoice as you normally would if those items were to be charged out to a customer. However your labour rates and other prices would usually be charged at cost price. To achieve this you can use one of the price structures in the stock file (price 1  6) as cost price and use the Parts Multipliers area to set that price to cost. You can also set the labour rate for the customer to the cost rate in the customer masterfiles.

 

Now make sure you have a customer (usually yourself under your own company name) setup for the internal work.

 

NOTE: Remember to only set the customer status to internal if you choose to use the 1st method of internal offset accounts and not using this method.

 

Start a job for that customer and invoice the parts, labour and revenue as normal. Then manually invoice a negative (-) amount against an offset account for the purpose of the invoice. Eg. Account 5100  Workshop cleaning. Or Account 9130  Motor Vehicle Expenses. (Note that accounts are suggestions only). The negative should be equal to the total of the invoice. This will make the actual invoice value 0.00 and therefore the customer will not incur a debt, the stock and labour is accounted for and the expense is accounted for all at the same time.

 

You may need to use the General Ledger Masks to allow you access to the offset accounts when manually selecting these accounts.
