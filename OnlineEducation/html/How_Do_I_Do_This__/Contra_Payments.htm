Contra Payments

 

Sometimes your customer and supplier is one in the same. You do work for a customer who also supplies goods and services to you. In the event that you both owe money to each other then you may chose to contra these amounts rather that actually sending each other a cheque. Or the one who owes the most amount simply sends a cheque for the difference and contras the rest of the money.

 

Firstly you need to setup a Contra offset account. This should be in an acount range between 1010 and 1019. Lets say 1016  Contra Offset account.

 

You then should go into the Payment Methods area under the Others > Maintenance area and setup a Payment Method called Contra which is linked to the 1016 account.

 

Lets say your Customer FRED01 owes you $1000 and you owe supplier FRED02 (the same entity but as a supplier with a different code) $1200.

 

You are now only going to pay FRED02 $200 and offset the $1000 balance that FRED01 owes to you.

 

Pay the $200 as for any other payment through the supplier payments area.

 

NOTE: If FRED01 owes you more money then the process is the same but you would receive the money from FRED01 using the customer payments option).

 

Now for the Contra

 

In the customer payments area, receive a payment from FRED01 for the $1000 contra amount and use the Contra payment method which is linked to the 1016 contra account.

 

Then make a supplier payment to FRED02 using the method as the 1016 Contra account.

 

This leaves the 1016 Contra account with a zero balance (no money actually changed hands) and also reduces to amount owed to FRED02 and owed by FRED01 by $1000, leaving the correct balance for both.

 

NOTE: While doing a credit return against both the customer and supplier for $1000 will achieve the correct balance on the appropriate accounts, it will distort both your sales and purchases figures and also cause your GST reconciliation to be inaccurate.

 

It is also advised not to use 1010 or any other normal payment account for processing contra payments as this will confuse the normal process of bank deposits and reconciling your other money received accounts.
