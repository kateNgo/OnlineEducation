Yellow Pages  Gst not 1/11th

 

Yellow Pages invoice, insurance invoices etc often provide a situation where the GSt is not 1/11th of the invoice total. In the case of yellow pages they pre-charge the GST for the whole period of the advertising agreement and then take payments for the remaining amount over a 6 month or other agreed period. In the first payment you pay the 1st of 6 installments at the ex gst rate plus the entire gst for all 6 installments. In this scenario you must process the invoice in one of the following ways.

 

Method 1. Process a purchase for the entire cost of the advertising in which case the GST will in fact be 1/11th of the total amount. This will leave an amount owing on your yellow pages creditors account of the total advertising bill. You can then pay the amount of each installment (with the first amount paying the whole gst value and the rest at the lower ex gst amount). If you are on a cash basis then you can only claim 1/11th of each payment of the gst and as such you will only get the full GST credit when the whole account is paid. If you are on an accrual basis then you can claim the entire GST at the beginning.

 

Method 2. Firstly process the first payment including GST on the invoice then process a purchase line including GST for 11 times the remaining GST Amount. This will create a gst amount of 1/11th of the total which is the gst value you require. Eg. To get a GST value of $100 process a purchase line of $1100.

 

Then process a negative amount of 10 times the gst as a No GST amount. This means you process a line for -$1000. The amount left on the purchase is $100 and it is all GST.

 

NOTE: Never process a purchase directly into the 2999 gst account. It may get a correct balance on the 2999 account but the gst reporting and the bas will be incorrect. The reason we must do it as above is so that the total sales including gst is 11 * the gst amount (sales $1100 and gst $100). Any other way will not result in the gst being 1/11th of the sale.
