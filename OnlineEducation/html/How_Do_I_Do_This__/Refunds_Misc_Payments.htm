Refunds & Misc Payments

 

There are many circumstances where you get money from misc sources rather than from customers. For example you may get money from Bank Interest, Insurance rebates, tax refunds, Rent from a premises the business owns etc. Firstly you need to setup an income account for the money received. This would usually be in the 8000 account series (lets say 8040  Refund received). In this case the way the entry is processed will depend on 2 things.

 

Does the amount include a GST component and do I want the money to appear in my bank deposit screen.

 

1. If you answered yes to either of the above questions then you must do an invoice and receive a payment for the money received. In the case of a refund that has a gst component, do an invoice for the refund and then receive the payment.

 

2. If there is no gst on the money and you donwant it to appear on the deposit screen then you can simply do a Journal Entry for the money. Credit the income received account (say 8040) and debit either the cash (1010) or the bank (1020) account.
