Overseas Purchases

 

When goods are purchased from an overseas supplier, the overseas supplier doesnactually charge the GST. The GST is charged by customs or through the freight company that that delivers the goods. In this case you have an invoice which has freight with a gst component and also has GST as a separate component with no actual expense against it as the expense was incurred from the overseas suppliers invoice.

 

Firstly you need to setup the overseas supplier in the Suppliers Masterfiles. Make sure that the ABN number is left blank and the Overseas Supplier option is ticked.

 

Processing the Order.

 

Produce a purchase order for the goods to be supplied from the overseas supplier. The system will automatically charge the goods at the EX GST price as the supplier is an overseas supplier and as such will not charge you GST. Process the order from the supplier in the usual way.

 

NOTE: Because the supplier is tagged as an overseas supplier the 48.5% withholding tax will not be deducted from the payment due to having no ABN number.

 

Processing the Freight / GST invoice.

 

When the freight / gst invoice arrives you will have certain charges on it. Go to the Purchase Order module and create a new order for the freight / gst invoice.

 

Duty Component

 

If there is duty applicable to the goods then purchase the duty to a duty account which is setup as a NO GST account.

 

Freight Component

 

The freight will almost invariably have a gst component of its own. Purchase the freight into a freight account as you normally would.

 

GST Component

 

You are now left with a gst only amount. If the total gst includes the freight component of the gst then you must adjust for this amount as it has already been taken care of when the freight line was entered.

 

NOTE: If the total GST was $110 and the gst on the freight was $10 then only $100 worth of gst must be processed in the following way.

 

Process a purchase line including GST for 11 times the GST Amount. This will create a gst amount of 1/11th of the total which is the gst value you require. Eg. To get a GST value of $100 process a purchase line of $1100.

 

Then process a negative amount of 10 times the gst as a No GST amount. This means you process a line for -$1000. The amount left on the purchase is $100 and it is all GST.

 

NOTE: Never process a purchase directly into the 2999 gst account. It may get a correct balance on the 2999 account but the gst reporting and the bas will be incorrect. The reason we must do it as above is so that the total sales including gst is 11 * the gst amount (sales $1100 and gst $100). Any other way will not result in the gst being 1/11th of the sale.

 

Proportion Invoice.

 

You are then able to proportion the invoice if you chose to do so which will adjust the cost of stock purchased on the original suppliers invoice by the freight, duty and gst amounts. The change in the cost of stock is made proportionately to the value of stock purchased. This is done by ticking the proportion invoice tick box before finalising the purchase order.
