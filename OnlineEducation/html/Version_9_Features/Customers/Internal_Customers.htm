Internal Customers

 

Internal Customer Invoicing

 

Sometimes you need to invoice parts and labour and other charges to a job item or customer which is in fact owned by you. This will occur when you work on your own job item or you have your own fleet of vehicles / assets that you maintain. You may also use internal customers for warranty, advertising, sponsorship and a number of other reasons. When you charge yourself for the goods and services provided to yourself it seems pointless to have to also pay yourself and then pay GST on your payment, which you then claim back. Sounds complicated? Well it was until now.

 

If you are in the situation above then you should set up a customer who is an internal customer. This customer will be your own company name. In the customer status field change the status to internal. You are then required to set up the offset ledgers which are used to offset your invoice against. There are 3 offset ledgers to be considered and they can be entered from either the Customer Masterfiles or the Job Item Masterfiles.

 

The offset settings in the Job Item files takes preference over the Customer Masterfile settings. If you manage a fleet of vehicles you may want certain type of vehicles to be offset to different accounts than others. You may want service vehicles offset against to different accounts than spare parts or admin vehicles for example. Or cars offset differently to forklifts.

 

There are 3 offset accounts to configure. There is the parts offset, which is used to offset parts charges on an invoice, a labour offset and a revenue offset against any revenue items charged. This means that if the invoice has $100 in parts, $60 labour and $20 revenue, the offset accounts will get a negative value corresponding to the amount of each section.

 

For example a -$100 will be put against the parts offset account. This means the actual invoice posting will be zero ($0.00) and no GST will apply. The individual parts, labour and revenue charges will process correctly and be offset in the ledger against the appropriate offset expense accounts such as Vehicle Maintenance etc. If you wish you can offset all sections (Parts, Labour and Revenue) against the same ledger account.
