Service Due Module

 

The Service Due Module allows you to generate service due letters, emails or SMS for customers that have services due on their job item.

 

The service due module requires that the customer has previously logged a service due date against the job item. When opening the service due module you are initially required to enter the date range for the service due.

 

You can then optionally select other filtering criteria such as the customer group, territory or mail group or the type of service that needs to be done. You are then able to click on the service due button to load all services due which have not already had a letter sent and fit into the filtering criteria selected. This will then load the list with all entries ticked to be sent. You can then either leave them ticked or untick the items that you do not wish to send service due reminders to.

 

You are then able to click on the Letter button to print letters or the Email button to send emails or the SMS button to send an SMS to the tagged list. Items that the service due is sent to or letters printed for are automatically flagged with a letter sent date of today's date.

 

Only emails with a valid email address or SMS with a valid customer mobile number will be flagged as sent. When clicking the service due button, items that are due for service within the selected criteria but where a letter has already been sent will not be displayed in the list.

 

You are then able to review a list of all of the letters sent within a date range that have not been followed up by selecting the date range and other filter criteria and clicking on the sent button.

 

This will fill the list with all of the letters that have been sent and allow you to resend reminder or follow up letters, email and SMS. If a follow-up has already been sent, then the item will not appear in the sent list.

 

You can also review items where a follow up has been sent by selecting the required criteria and clicking on the follow-up buton.

 

To avoid items continuing to appear in the service due module once the vehicle is brought in for a service, the user should delete from the service due list the service that is booked in at the time a new service is booked into the system. This can be done at the time of booking in the service.

 

The clear button will allow you to clear the current list and filter criteria which will then allow you to use a new set of criteria to filter on and generate service reminders.
