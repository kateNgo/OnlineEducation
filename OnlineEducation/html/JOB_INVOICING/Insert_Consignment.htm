Insert Consignment
Consignment stock refers to large items on consignment such as motors, outboard marine motors, motor bikes, lawn mowers etc. This is not used for items such as consignment batteries.
Choose Cons  F6 button or press the F6 key to insert consignment items in the record. The consignment window appears showing all consignment stock currently on hand. Sold items will not appear in the list. Select the Consignment Item Code and or left click to highlight the item and then choose Select to confirm the selected item. The item can be selected by serial number, make or model depending on the TAB selected at the top of the window.

 

Discount Rate - Enter percentage allowed for item discount for this customer, if applicable.

 

Qty  The consignment qty can only be a qty of 1. This is selected by the serial number or rego number for cars/bikes etc.

 

Price  Enter the Price per part. This price is automatically selected from the consignment masterfile pricing depending on the price rate set up for this customer which is set in the customer masterfiles. This price can be manually overridden for each item.

 

Units  Unit of Stock item (automatically inserted). These come from the Consignment Masterfiles and are texts such as EACH, SET, PAIR etc.

 

Extension  The total price of the number of parts (automatically calculated). You can change the extension and it will automatically recalculate the unit price.

 

Less Discount  The amount to be discounted (automatically inserted). Calculated from the discount rate.

 

GST  The amount of GST that the part attracts (automatically calculated). This rate is set from the control system. If this is an export invoice then the rate will be zero.

 

Total  The total amount to be invoiced for the part, less any discount.

 

Note:  The total amount displayed includes the GST. Do not try to take off the discount and then add the GST as you will not get the correct result. The total is the qty * unit price less discount and the GST is calculated from the total.

 

C  Left click on the C button to view the cost price of the part per unit.

 

Choose More, if another part record is to be added and repeat the insert stock procedure. You can also press the ENTER key which acts as a more button to add further items.

 

Choose OK or the F12 key, if there are no more parts to be added to the record.
