Job Header

 

Job Header Details

 

Progressive Invoicing - This option allows you to either progressively invoice a job over a period of time or create different invoices for different customers from one job. This is useful for doing an invoice where the customer, and insurance company and also a warranty company all pay part of a job. It can also be used to store customer orders and Stock on the invoice can be finalised as the stock is supplied. See Progressive Invoicing for more help.

 

Job Description  The Description defaults to GENERAL WORK but can be changed by the user. You can either manually type the Job Description or you may wish to select it from a list of standard job descriptions that you are able to create. To select a description from a list or add a new Standard Job Description left click on the button to the right of the description field.

 

Bill To - Enter the Customer Code to whom the bill is to be sent to, by clicking the button on the right of the to field in the Job Invoicing main window. (See Job Items section or customer masterfiles section to change customer to whom bill is to be sent). The bill to allows you to charge the invoice/job to someone who is not the owner of the Job Item such as an insurance company or other third party.

 

When the billto customer is changed on an invoice, a message is displayed suggesting that the user Reprices the invoice. This is because the billto customer may have a different price rate or discount structure to the previous billto customer. You may also be changing the billto from an internal customer that is charged at cost price to a external customer that is charged at retail. You should then choose Yes to change the invoice prices to be appropriate for the new billto customer.

 

Order No  Enter order number provided by Customer. This can be an alphanumeric number. You can also use this as a general reference number or to record a number from a manual docket book. The order number can be inserted or updated using the F4 key from Job History after a job is finalised as can the meter reading and job description.

 

Estimated Hours  The estimated Hours field allows you to enter the estimated time it will take to complete the selected job. This is then used in the workshop diary module to calculate the available chargeable hours for any given day.

 

Time - This field stores the time that the job was booked in based on the computer system time which records both the time that the job is due to be finished and also the time it actually was finished. This information can then be viewed from the workshop diary to make it easier to organise work and ensure jobs are completed on time.

 

Booking Date  Enter date of job / invoice. You can also use the date Calendar to select the date. This is the date the job is booked in. The invoice defaults to the current date when it is finalised but can be changed to any date you wish. If you change the date to a date outside of the current month then it will still be processed for accounting reasons in the current month but may cause confusion when reports such as the invoice register is printed within a date range. It may also cause confusion as it will not show on today at a glance until the finalised date is reached. If an invoice is backdated then the day at a glance figures are backdated as well and will not appear for the current date.

 

For Export Left click, to mark box to show that the part is to be sent overseas. This can also be used for any sale where NO GST is to be calculated for the sale. You can use this when a part of an invoice is exempt. If one item is exempt then tick the export box, enter the exempt item, and then untick the export box for the remaining items.

 

Mark Up - The Mark Up field next to the Customer name allows you to change the default markup set from the Customer Masterfiles for the selected customer. This then causes the software to mark up Stock on the invoice by a percentage above the standard price rate for the selected customer.

 

Salesman / Status. - The blank field above the Export field, with a button next to it is the SalesmanField. If you left click on the button a window with a list of your sales people will appear. You can select which sales person is to be inserted. You can add, change, delete sales people by going to the Salesman File in Group Maintenance. This option can also be used as a job status to select a job by status and print a job status report. Eg. Status 01 = Quoted, 02 = waiting for Stock, 03 = waiting for customer approval etc.

 

Stock Disc  Enter percentage allowed for Stock discount for this customer, if applicable. This discount will be automatically updated from the customer masterfiles for the selected customer if that customer has a set Stock discount.

 

Labour Disc  Enter percentage allowed for labour discount for this customer, or clicking on the up and down arrows, if applicable. This discount will be automatically updated from the customer masterfiles for the selected customer if that customer has a set labour discount. You cannot set a labour rate and a discount rate for the same customer. It is one or the other.

 

Rev Disc  Enter percentage allowed for Rev Discount for this customer, or clicking on the up and down arrows, if applicable. This discount will be automatically updated from the customer masterfiles for the selected customer.

 

Price Rate  Enter the Price Rate by clicking the menu and selecting (Retail, 2-6), or clicking on the up and down arrows, if applicable. This price rate will be automatically updated from the customer masterfiles for the selected customer. The price rate defaults to rate 1, which is retail. In the stock masterfiles there are 4 base price rates as well as a recommended retail price for each part.

 

Note: A particular stock item can have 6 different selling prices (1,2,3,4,5,6). By selecting a Stock Price, at the time of invoicing the customer, a particular set price applies to this customer. Price 1 is the highest (usually retail) price. Price 2 is the second highest (usually trade) and price 3, 4, 5 and 6 are further discounted prices. The recommended retail price can be printed on the invoice. Whether or not to print the recommended price for the invoice can be set from the customer masterfile and changed in the print window after finalising an invoice or when printing a proforma invoice.

 

Job No.  A job number will be automatically allocated to the job and will increase by 1 for each subsequent job. The last job number can be increased but not decreased in the control system section. You can also manually enter a job number prior to saving the job for the first time. This allows job numbers to be used as per a manual job numbering system.

 

Note  the date automatically defaults to the day the job is file is created.

ABN - The customerABN number is automatically inserted into the ABN field.

 

Pages- The pages field indicates the number of PRINTED pages that will be used for the current invoice. The number of lines available per page is usually 27 lines depending on the invoice format which is used. This may vary for some printers.

COD - A red COD message will appear next to the ABN field if the customer has zero terms set and therefore is a COD customer.

 

Serial Number.  The serial number field displays the serial number of a stock item if that item is a serialised stock item.

 

Change Job Item Owner at post - If a customer is flagged in the customer masterfiles as being a Internal Customer and the BILLTO is different to the customer in the invoice module then a tick box is available allowing the owner and billto of the Job item to be changed to the Billto code in the Job Item masterfiles. The change will only take place if the tick is selected. This function is used when a vehicle or lawn mover or boat or other such item belonging to the business is sold to a customer and hence the billto customer will become the owner of that job item from now on.
