Finance Button

 

Package Costs listbox displays a list of any direct costs associated with this Item.

 

Cheque Entry button opens up the cheque entry screen for you to enter payment details on the fly against the selected sales order, To enter cheque entry details into this screen, click on the date button to chose your date, click on the method dropdown listbox and chose your method (eg either bank or cash). Then tab to the Ledger button to chose your ledger and then enter the cheque number, description and amount details by typing them into the fields. Then click on the Item button to chose your Item and then select what type this cheque entry is (eg either expense, fuel, tyres, curtailment or interest). Then click on Process button to complete the transaction entry.

 

The Estimated Costs listbox allows you to add any fictitious costs that may be associated with this sale. These costs are only used to estimate expenses and profit margins in the event that they were incurred but do not actually form part of the true sales costs. You may wish to add in this area things that you might give to the prospect free of charge or add to the sale expenses you may incur over and above anything that has already been incurred. This will then affect the profit on the screen below but will not effect the actual profit which gets posted to the ledger. Once a sale is finalised these estimated costs are removed from the list.

 

Under the Profit Details the system shows in summary form, how much profit was made on the packaged deal, the accessories and on costs. It also shows the overtrade and any discount amounts as well as sales commission. It then shows the Total profit for this sale.

 

Journals

By clicking the third icon from the right hand side of your screen you can access the Journal entry screen. To enter a new journal entry just click on the insert button and you will be in insert mode.

 

For Export

If you are making an overseas sale, click in the For Export tick box before adding any charges on the sale.
