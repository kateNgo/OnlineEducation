Trade In Details

 

Trade in ID  this is the trade in identification which would usually be a registration or serial number that could be a stock number that you assign to this trade in so it can be later identified when being serviced or sold. If the ID is left blank then it will be automatically assigned to the next item number available when it is processed.

 

MnF Year - allows you to select the manufacturing year date. To select the Manufacturing year date, click on the MnF Year button and a calendar will appear. Use the arrows to select the month and year and select the date by clicking on the preferred date number.

 

Job Item  this is where you insert the registration details on the Item.

 

Trans  this is where you indicate the transmission of the Item. Enter either manual or automatic.

 

Make/Model  you can manually type the make and model of the Item into these fields or you can click on the make and model button which gives you a list of all the makes and models. To select your make and model, use the arrow keys to scroll down (or use the mouse to scroll down) and left click with the mouse on your preferred make. Then click on the model options and using the arrows to scroll down (or use the mouse to scroll down) and left click to select your preferred model. Then click on the select button on the bottom of the window to insert the details into the trading screen.

 

Colour field this is where you insert the colour of the item.

 

Cylinders  this is where you insert the number of cylinders the Item runs on.

 

Engine and Chassis  this is where you can record the details of the Itemengine and chassis numbers.

 

Click on the Save button at the bottom of the Trade In information.

 

Trade Type  the trade type can be either personal or business. This affects how GST is calculated. On business trade ins the supplier will provide you with a tax invoice and you will be able to claim the GST immediately according to your normal GST processes. If the trade in is personal the it follows the second hand GST law which means that GST on the trade in can only be claimed once the item is sold.

 

Acquired Date  the Acquired Date will be automatically entered into this field and the time of finalising the sale.

 

Appraisal ID  the Appraisal ID field is a reference number if required for a reference which relates to the appraisal or valuation of the trade in.

 

Appraisal date  this allows you to enter the date the appraisal for the trade in was done.

 

Valuation $ - this is where you record the valuation amount of the trade in.

 

Requested  this field allows you to enter the amount that was requested by the person offering the trade in for the item.

 

Trade Given  this indicates the amount given to the customer for the trade in.

 

Over Trade  this field is the amount given less the amount valued for the Item. If there is no valuation amount, the system assumes that the trade in given and valuation is the same. This is used to calculate actual Item value as opposed to the amount given against the trade in.

 

Meter Reading  this is where you can record the odometer reading from the trade in at the time the item was traded in.

 

Picture  this is where you can insert a picture or image of the trade in Item. To insert a picture, click on the picture button then the plant picture screen appears, click on the picture file button and the bottom of the screen and choose your image via the windows directory. Then click on open to insert the image in the picture screen. Then click on OK to save.

 

Notes  this is where you can record any other details relevant to the trade in.

 

Process  the process button processes the trade in transaction and all associated ledger records. It also adds the trade in into your Item file and makes it available for sale at a later date. This process does all of the accounting required to correctly evaluate your GST and other accounting requirements.

 

Revs ref  if you have done a REVcheck then this is the REVreference number assigned when the REVcheck was done. This information will then be transposed to the Item Register, Accounts Tab.

 

Revs date  this is the date the REVcheck was done.

 

Revs time  this is the time that the REVcheck was done.

 

Notes  this is where you can record any other information in relation to that specific Item.

 

Finance Co  this is the finance company that the trade in is currently encumbered with if any.

 

Account No.  this is the account number of the finance company as above.

 

Fleet ID - this is the identification the finance company has for this particular item.

 

Payout  this is the amount of the payout required to settle the current finance against this trade in item.

 

Payout date  this allows you to select the date that any provided payout figure is calculated to.

 

Notes  these notes refer to any comments you require regarding the finance or payout figures.
